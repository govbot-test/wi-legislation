andWhereas, as a result of the attacks, key Iranian officials have been killed, including Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, the Supreme Leader of Iran, who ruled Iran as a brutal dictator for 36 years; andWhereas, Iran responded by launching widespread missile and drone counter attacks against Israel, neighboring Arab states, and U.S. military bases in the region; andWhereas, over 400 members of Wisconsin’s Army National Guard are stationed in the Middle East, operating in Kuwait and Iraq, and supporting key operations of the U.S. strikes on Iran; andWhereas, as of March 15, 2026, seven members of the U.S. Armed Forces have been killed and approximately 140 service members have been wounded in military offensives, and an additional seven individuals have died in the course of their service from non-combat-related causes; andWhereas, the U.S. government has released information about the deceased: Sergeant First Class Nicole M. Amor, Sergeant Declan J. Coady, Captain Cody A. Khork, Chief Warrant Officer 3 Robert M. Marzan, Major Jeffrey R. O’Brien, and Sergeant First Class Noah L. Tietjens, who were assigned to the 103rd Sustainment Command based in Des Moines, Iowa; andWhereas, the U.S. Central Command has announced that these service members were killed in an Iranian strike on a base housing American troops in Kuwait; andWhereas, Sergeant Benjamin N. Pennington, of the 1st Space Battalion of the 1st Space Brigade in Fort Carson, Colorado, died after being wounded during an Iranian strike on an air base in Saudi Arabia; andWhereas, Major Sorffly Davius, of the New York Army National Guard 42nd Infantry Division, died of non-combat-related causes while deployed as a part of Operation Epic Fury in Kuwait; andWhereas, Major John A. Klinner, Technical Sergeant Ashley B. Pruitt, and Captain Ariana G. Savino of the 6th Air Refueling Wing of the MacDill Air Force Base in Florida and Captain Curtis J. Angst, Captain Seth R. Koval, and Technical Sergeant Tyler H. Simmons of the 121st Air Refueling Wing at Rickenbacker Air National Guard Base in Columbus, Ohio, perished in a crash of their KC-135 Stratotanker aircraft in Iraq; andWhereas, the circumstances of the incident are currently under investigation and the U.S. Central Command has stated the crash was not the result of hostile or friendly fire; andWhereas, Americans will remember the bravery of these fallen service members who suffered injury and lost their lives during the involvement of the United States in Iran; now, therefore, be itResolved by the senate, That the members of the Wisconsin State Senate honor the memory and service of these fallen service members and extend their deepest condolences to the families, friends, and colleagues of these fallen service members; and, be it furtherResolved, That the members of the Wisconsin State Senate extend their deepest gratitude to the American personnel who are still in harm’s way during this ongoing conflict; their service is of immeasurable value to our nation.
