Referred to Committee on Senate Organization.SJR148,1,21Relating to: commending employers that permit employees to leave work to 2perform volunteer emergency medical services duties.SJR148,1,53Whereas, emergency medical services (EMS) are a vital component of our 4healthcare system, providing lifesaving medical care to patients during times of 5crisis; andSJR148,1,76Whereas, volunteers are critical to EMS operations across the state, especially 7in small and rural communities; andSJR148,1,108Whereas, about 76 percent of active EMS service providers licensed in 9Wisconsin are primarily staffed by volunteers, per the Wisconsin Department of 10Health Services; andSJR148,1,1311Whereas, a state survey of EMS agencies licensed in Wisconsin whose 12primary service was 911 transport found that a majority of responding agencies 13had “volunteer model” crews, of which 72 percent were rural agencies; andSJR148,2,214Whereas, EMS agencies face particular challenges with staffing during the 1daytime, when many volunteers are unavailable due to commitments at their 2primary place of employment; andSJR148,2,83Whereas, current state law generally requires an employer to allow an 4employee who is a volunteer firefighter, EMS practitioner, emergency medical 5responder, or ambulance driver for a volunteer fire department or fire company, 6public agency, or nonprofit corporation to be late for or absent from work if the 7employee is responding to an emergency that begins before the employee is required 8to report for work; andSJR148,2,119Whereas, current state law, however, does not require an employer to allow 10such an employee to leave work to respond to an emergency that occurs during 11work; andSJR148,2,1512Whereas, given the widespread need for EMS volunteers in this state, 13employers that permit their employees to leave work to perform EMS duties during 14an emergency—despite not being required to—should be lauded for enabling rapid 15emergency responses; now, therefore, be itSJR148,2,1916Resolved by the senate, the assembly concurring, That the members of 17the Wisconsin Legislature commend employers in this state that permit their 18employees to leave work to perform volunteer EMS duties in response to an 19emergency.SJR148,2,2020(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/sjr148 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/sjr148 proposaltext/2025/REG/SJR148 proposaltext/2025/REG/SJR148 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»SJR148: Joint Resolution Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
