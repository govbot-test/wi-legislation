2025 ASSEMBLY RESOLUTION 14
February 18, 2026 - Introduced by Committee on Rules.
AR14,1,2
1Relating to: establishing special orders of business for Thursday, February 19,
22026.
AR14,1,33Resolved by the assembly, That:
AR14,1Section 1. Special order scheduled. The following proposals shall be special orders of business on Thursday, February 19, 2026, at the times shown:
(1)   At 11:01 a.m., 2025 Assembly Joint Resolution 127, or 2025 Senate Joint Resolution 116, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: prohibiting the governor from using the partial veto to create or increase any tax or fee (second consideration).
(2)   At 11:02 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 51, or 2025 Senate Bill 16, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: participation in interscholastic athletics and application of the public records and open meetings laws to interscholastic athletic associations.
(3)   At 11:03 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 97, or 2025 Senate Bill 23, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: extension of eligibility under the Medical Assistance program for postpartum women.
(4)   At 11:04 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 117, relating to: hunting of sandhill cranes, programs related to sandhill crane damage, providing an exemption from emergency rule procedures, granting rule-making authority, and making an appropriation.
(5)   At 11:05 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 130, relating to: exempting certain persons from PFAS enforcement actions under the spills law.
(6)   At 11:06 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 131, relating to: programs and requirements to address PFAS.
(7)   At 11:07 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 180, relating to: requiring the Department of Health Services to seek any necessary waiver to prohibit the purchase of candy or soft drinks with FoodShare benefits.
(8)   At 11:08 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 192, relating to: fatality review teams and granting rule-making authority.
(9)   At 11:09 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 197, or 2025 Senate Bill 181, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: a levy limit exemption for regional emergency medical systems and eligibility for the expenditure restraint incentive program.
(10)   At 11:10 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 208, or 2025 Senate Bill 176, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: an income and franchise tax exemption for broadband expansion grants and for federal high-cost program funding for broadband expansion.
(11)   At 11:11 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 241, relating to: required ratio of journeyworkers to apprentices in apprenticeship programs and contracts.
(12)   At 11:12 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 248, relating to: vacancies in appointive state offices.
(13)   At 11:13 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 263, or 2025 Senate Bill 264, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: coverage of breast cancer screenings by the Medical Assistance program and health insurance policies and plans.
(14)   At 11:14 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 283, or 2025 Senate Bill 291, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: making certain child care expenditures eligible for the business development tax credit.
(15)   At 11:15 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 326, relating to: local grant writing and compliance assistance.
(16)   At 11:16 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 387, relating to: correction bill for 2025 Wisconsin Act 15 and making an appropriation.
(17)   At 11:17 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 391, or 2025 Senate Bill 389, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: the per pupil adjustment for school district revenue limits.
(18)   At 11:18 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 418, or 2025 Senate Bill 417, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: visitation of a long-term care facility resident or hospital patient during a communicable disease outbreak.
(19)   At 11:19 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 421, or 2025 Senate Bill 420, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: prohibiting rights of nature ordinances.
(20)   At 11:20 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 430, or 2025 Senate Bill 431, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: discrimination based on arrest record under the fair employment law.
(21)   At 11:21 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 436, relating to: requiring animal testing facilities and breeders to offer certain dogs and cats for adoption to releasing agencies and providing a penalty.
(22)   At 11:22 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 454, relating to: a workforce home loan program.
(23)   At 11:23 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 460, relating to: pupil eligibility for parental choice programs based upon participation by a sibling or a dependent child of the pupil's parent or guardian.
(24)   At 11:24 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 505, or 2025 Senate Bill 501, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: creating a veterinary loan repayment grant program administered by the Higher Educational Aids Board and exempting from taxation amounts received from such a grant program.
(25)   At 11:25 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 514, relating to: authorizing additional circuit court and criminal justice system positions.
(26)   At 11:26 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 601, relating to: excluding certain event or sports wagers from the definition of “bet”.
(27)   At 11:27 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 659, relating to: tuition and fee remission or grants for certain veterans and their dependents enrolled in the University of Wisconsin System, a technical college, or a private nonprofit institution of higher education; and the University of Wisconsin System nonresident tuition exemption for certain veterans.
(28)   At 11:28 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 681, or 2025 Senate Bill 678, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: the use of oral fluids for preliminary screening of a person suspected of operating a motor vehicle while intoxicated.
(29)   At 11:29 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 742, relating to: regulation of lease-purchase agreements for personal property and creating a cause of action.
(30)   At 11:30 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 806, or 2025 Senate Bill 799, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: parental access to health records of minors.
(31)   At 11:31 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 807, relating to: office of county board counsel in certain counties.
(32)   At 11:32 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 812, relating to: allowing more than 35 percent of the area within a tax incremental district in the Village of Somers to be used for residential use.
(33)   At 11:33 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 819, or 2025 Senate Bill 822, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: allowing discounts for prompt payment of health care fees.
(34)   At 11:34 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 837, or 2025 Senate Bill 814, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: placement of sexually violent persons on supervised release.
(35)   At 11:35 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 839, relating to: allowing the Town of Three Lakes in Oneida County to create a tax incremental district in the same manner as a city or village.
(36)   At 11:36 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 842, or 2025 Senate Bill 832, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: remote dispensing sites under the pharmacy practice law.
(37)   At 11:37 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 866, relating to: the administration of the office of county sheriff and the appointment of county medical examiners.
(38)   At 11:38 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 870, relating to: adopting changes to the federal Internal Revenue Code for state tax purposes and modifying administrative rules related to such changes.
(39)   At 11:39 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 874, or 2025 Senate Bill 825, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: environmental review prerequisites for certain major highway project actions.
(40)   At 11:40 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 878, relating to: a tax credit for certain sales and use taxes paid on services sold through an amusement device.
(41)   At 11:41 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 880, relating to: nuisance actions against racing facilities.
(42)   At 11:42 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 892, relating to: digital asset staking and the state’s securities laws.
(43)   At 11:43 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 894, relating to: terms and conditions of gift certificates, gift cards, and other gift obligations and providing a penalty.
(44)   At 11:44 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 903, relating to: termination of parental rights under the safe haven law.
(45)   At 11:45 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 912, or 2025 Senate Bill 884, if it is in the Assembly, relating to: an audiovisual recording of a child’s statement admitted as evidence.
(46)   At 11:46 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 913, relating to: subtraction for certain income of members of the National Guard and Reserves.
(47)   At 11:47 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 918, relating to: required subjects in a human growth and development instructional program.
(48)   At 11:48 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 923, relating to: creating a civil cause of action for sexual extortion.
(49)   At 11:49 a.m., 2025 Assembly Bill 948, relating to: policies to prohibit the use of wireless communication devices during the school day in public schools.
