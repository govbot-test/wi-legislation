2025 ASSEMBLY BILL 948
January 28, 2026 - Introduced by Representatives Brill, Kitchens, Dittrich, O'Connor, Armstrong, Donovan, Nedweski, Piwowarczyk, Murphy, Wichgers, Neylon and Knodl, cosponsored by Senator Cabral-Guevara. Referred to Committee on Education.
AB948,1,4
1An Act to repeal 120.12 (29); to amend 115.28 (38), 119.04 (1) and 120.12 (29)
2(title); to create 120.12 (30) of the statutes; relating to: policies to prohibit
3the use of wireless communication devices during the school day in public
4schools.
Analysis by the Legislative Reference Bureau
2025 Wisconsin Act 42 requires each school board to adopt, by July 1, 2026, a policy that generally prohibits pupils from using wireless communication devices during instructional time. The policy must define a “wireless communication device” as a portable wireless device that is capable of providing voice, messaging, or other data communication between two or more parties and must include cellular phones, tablet computers, laptop computers, and gaming devices. The policy must include exceptions to the general prohibition against using wireless communication devices during instructional time 1) for emergencies and perceived threats, 2) to manage a pupil’s health care, 3) for a use included in an individualized education program or 504 plan, and 4) for a use authorized by a teacher for educational purposes and may include other exceptions if the school board determines that doing so is beneficial for pupil education or well-being.
Beginning on July 1, 2027, this bill replaces that wireless communication device policy requirement with a requirement that each school board adopt a policy that generally prohibits pupils from using wireless communication devices during the school day. Under the bill, “school day” includes instructional time, recess, the time pupils transfer between classes, and the lunch period on each day on which school is in session. The bill generally includes the same requirements and exceptions for wireless communication device policies as Act 42. The bill adds that a wireless communication device policy may include the use of pouches or other storage devices for wireless communication devices or a prohibition on possession of a wireless communication device on school premises.
For further information see the state and local fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.
