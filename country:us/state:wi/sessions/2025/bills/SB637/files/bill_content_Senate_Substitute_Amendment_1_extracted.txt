to create 71.07 (12), 71.10 (4) (fo), 71.28 3(12), 71.30 (3) (do), 71.47 (12), 71.49 (1) (do), 77.54 (76), 238.30 (7) (f) and 4238.401 of the statutes; relating to: a sales and use tax exemption and an 5income and franchise tax credit for nuclear energy facilities.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill creates a sales and use tax exemption for tangible personal property sold to a construction contractor or nuclear project developer that, in fulfillment of a real property construction activity, becomes a component of a nuclear energy facility in this state. The bill defines a “nuclear energy facility” as electric generating equipment and associated facilities that derive electricity from nuclear power.The bill also allows an electric public utility, electric cooperative, municipal electric company, or qualified wholesale electric company to claim an income and franchise tax credit in an amount not to exceed 15 percent of the wages paid to full-time employees of a claimant’s nuclear energy facility in this state and and not to exceed 15 percent of the capital expenditures paid for the construction, improvement, renovation, repair, maintenance, development, or operation of a claimant’s nuclear energy facility in this state, not including expenditures for acquiring land. No person may claim the credit unless the person is certified to do so by the Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation and no person may claim the credit for more than five consecutive years.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB637-SSA1,11Section 1. 71.05 (6) (a) 15. of the statutes, as affected by 2025 Wisconsin Act 215, is amended to read:SB637-SSA1,2,8371.05 (6) (a) 15.
