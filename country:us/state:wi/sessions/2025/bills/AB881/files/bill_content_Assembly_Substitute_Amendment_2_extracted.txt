to create 31.16 of the 2statutes; relating to: water diversion or elevation projects in towns.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauUnder this bill, except in the case of an emergency, the Department of Natural Resources may not initiate, authorize, or issue a permit for a project located in a town that involves the use of stationary or portable pumps or other mechanisms that divert water to locations where water is not naturally occurring or that raise water elevation above what is naturally occurring unless the town board of the town in which the activity will occur approves the project by resolution. This limits DNR’s general authority under current law to regulate and control the level and flow of water in navigable waters and to divert the flow of water of a body of water for the purpose of flood control and prevention, including when such a diversion creates an artificial water body.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB881-ASA2,13Section 1. 30.18 (8) of the statutes is amended to read:AB881-ASA2,2,11130.18 (8) Department may raise water elevations. If after examination 2and investigation the department determines that it is necessary to raise water 3elevations in any navigable stream or lake for conservation purposes, the 4department may, if funds are available from any source other than license fees and 5subject to the requirement under s. 31.16, determine and establish the elevations to 6which the water may be raised or maintained, but the water elevation may not be 7established below the normal elevation. If any lands are damaged by raising the 8water levels above normal and the department cannot acquire the right to flow the 9lands by agreement with the owner, the department may, subject to the 10requirement under s. 31.16, acquire the lands or the right to flow the lands by 11condemnation under ch. 32.AB881-ASA2,212Section 2. 31.02 (1) of the statutes is amended to read:AB881-ASA2,2,241331.02 (1) The department, in the interest of public rights in navigable waters, 14to promote safety, and to protect life, health, property, property values, and 15economic values may, subject to the requirement under s. 31.16, regulate and 16control the level and flow of water in all navigable waters and may erect, or may 17order and require bench marks to be erected, upon which shall be designated the 18maximum level of water that may be impounded and the lowest level of water that 19may be maintained by any dam heretofore or hereafter constructed and maintained 20and which will affect the level and flow of navigable waters; and may by order fix a 21level for any body of navigable water below which the same shall not be lowered 22except as provided in this chapter; and shall establish and maintain gauging 23stations upon the various navigable waters of the state and shall take other steps 24necessary to determine and record the characteristics of such waters.AB881-ASA2,31Section 3. 31.16 of the statutes is created to read:AB881-ASA2,3,8231.16 Projects involving water diversion. Except in the case of an 3emergency, the department may not initiate, authorize, or issue a permit for a 4project located in a town that involves the use of stationary or portable pumps or 5other mechanisms that divert water to locations where water is not naturally 6occurring or that raise the water elevation of a body of water above what is naturally 7occurring unless the town board of the town in which the activity will occur 8approves the project by resolution.AB881-ASA2,49Section 4. 87.02 (1) of the statutes is amended to read:AB881-ASA2,3,151087.02 (1) To order the straightening, widening, altering, deepening, changing 11or the removing of obstructions from the course of any river, watercourse, pond, 12lake, creek, or natural stream, ditch, drain, or sewer, and the concentration, 13diversion, or division of the flow of water therein; provided, that in the case of 14navigable waters no such work shall substantially impair the navigability thereof 15and that any such project located in a town is subject to s. 31.16.AB881-ASA2,3,1616(end) Down Down /2025/related/amendments/ab881/asa2_ab881 true amends /2025/related/amendments/ab881/asa2_ab881 amends/2025/REG/AB881-ASA2 amends/2025/REG/AB881-ASA2 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Amendments»AB881»Amendment ASA2-AB881 × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
