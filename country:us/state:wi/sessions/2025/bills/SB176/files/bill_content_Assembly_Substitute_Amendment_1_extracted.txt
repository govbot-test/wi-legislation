The credit is equal to 6.32 percent of the qualified broadband expansion funding received by a claimant during a tax year. Under the bill, “qualified broadband expansion funding” is 1) a grant for broadband expansion issued by this state; a city, village, town, or county of this state; a tribal government in this state; or the federal government; or 2) funding from the the federal government for any high-cost universal service funding for broadband expansion. Current law provides an income and franchise tax exemption for income received in the form of allocations issued by this state with moneys received from the federal coronavirus relief fund to be used for broadband expansion. The credit taxes effect for tax year 2026 and sunsets after tax year 2030.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB176-ASA1,11Section 1. 71.05 (6) (a) 15. of the statutes, as affected by 2025 Wisconsin Act 215, is amended to read:SB176-ASA1,2,8371.05 (6) (a) 15.
