relating 2to: prohibiting critical telecommunications infrastructure from including 3equipment manufactured by certain entities, prohibiting certain contracts 4and uses of technology related to critical infrastructure, and providing a 5penalty.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB651-SSA1,16Section 1. 1.12 (5) (c) of the statutes is created to read:SB651-SSA1,2,271.12 (5) (c) It is the policy of this state that any network-connected 8information and communication technology that would allow a foreign principal, as 9defined in s. 16.14 (1) (d), to directly or remotely access or control critical 1infrastructure, as defined in s. 16.14 (1) (a), related to energy in this state should 2not be used in that critical infrastructure.SB651-SSA1,23Section 2. 16.14 of the statutes is created to read:SB651-SSA1,2,5416.14 Contracts and uses of technology related to critical 5infrastructure. (1) Definitions. In this section:SB651-SSA1,2,116(a) “Critical infrastructure” means systems and assets, whether physical or 7virtual or publicly or privately held, so vital to this state or to the United States that 8the incapacity or destruction of such systems and assets would have a debilitating 9effect on state or national security, state or national economic security, state or 10national public health, or any combination of the foregoing. “Critical 11infrastructure” includes all of the following:SB651-SSA1,2,12121. Gas and oil production, storage, and delivery systems.SB651-SSA1,2,13132. Water supply, refinement, storage, and delivery systems.SB651-SSA1,2,14143. Telecommunications networks.SB651-SSA1,2,15154. Electrical power delivery systems.SB651-SSA1,2,16165. Emergency services.SB651-SSA1,2,17176. Transportation systems and services.SB651-SSA1,2,1818(b) “Entity” means a person other than an individual.SB651-SSA1,2,2019(c) 1. Except as provided under subd. 2., “foreign adversary” means any of the 20following:SB651-SSA1,2,2221a. The People’s Republic of China, including the Hong Kong Special 22Administrative Region and the Macau Special Administrative Region.SB651-SSA1,2,2323b. The Republic of Cuba.SB651-SSA1,2,2424c. The Islamic Republic of Iran.SB651-SSA1,3,11d. The Democratic People’s Republic of Korea.SB651-SSA1,3,22e. The Russian Federation.SB651-SSA1,3,33f. The Venezuelan regime of Nicolás Maduro.SB651-SSA1,3,742. “Foreign adversary” does not include any country, government, regime, or 5person specified under subd. 1. if the country, government, regime, or person is not 6included in the list of foreign adversaries under 15 CFR 791.4, as amended from 7time to time.SB651-SSA1,3,88(d) “Foreign principal” means any of the following:SB651-SSA1,3,991. A foreign adversary or an agent of a foreign adversary.SB651-SSA1,3,10102. An individual who is a citizen of a country controlled by a foreign adversary.SB651-SSA1,3,13113. A business entity that is organized under the laws of a country controlled by 12a foreign adversary and whose principal place of business is located within territory 13controlled by that foreign adversary.SB651-SSA1,3,15144. An investment fund that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary or 15agent of a foreign adversary.SB651-SSA1,3,18165. An entity that has 50 percent or more of its stock, securities, or other 17indicia of ownership owned or controlled, directly or indirectly, by a foreign 18adversary or other person or combination of persons described in subds. 1. to 4.SB651-SSA1,3,24196. An entity whose governance structure or internal monitoring or human 20resources decision-making is consistent with the objectives set forth in the Opinion 21on Strengthening the United Front Work of the Private Economy in the New Era 22issued on September 15, 2020, by the General Office of the Central Committee of 23the Chinese Communist Party or a successor or similar document issued by the 24Chinese Communist Party by an agent of the Chinese Communist Party.SB651-SSA1,4,517. An entity that is controlled by the government of the People’s Republic of 2China, the Chinese Communist Party, the Chinese military, or an instrumentality 3of any of the foregoing, including the State-owned Assets Supervision and 4Administration Commission of the State Council of the People’s Republic of China 5and the National Council for Social Security Fund of the People’s Republic of China.SB651-SSA1,4,668. An agent of a person described in subds. 1. to 7.SB651-SSA1,4,107(e) “State agency” means an association, authority, board, department, 8commission, independent agency, institution, system, office, society, or other body 9in state government created or authorized to be created by the constitution or any 10law.SB651-SSA1,4,1211(2) Prohibited contracts and uses of technology. (a) Except as provided 12under par. (b), no state agency may do any of the following:SB651-SSA1,4,15131. Enter into a contract with a foreign principal that allows the foreign 14principal to directly or remotely access or control critical infrastructure in this 15state.SB651-SSA1,4,19162. Put into use in critical infrastructure any network-connected information 17and communication technology that allows a foreign principal to directly or 18remotely access or control critical infrastructure in this state and that appears on 19the list maintained by the department under sub. (3) (b).SB651-SSA1,4,2320(b) If a state agency is unable to source an alternative to a technology or entity 21on the list under sub. (3) (b), the state agency shall certify to the department each 22contract or use of a technology described in par. (a) 1. or 2. related to critical 23infrastructure within this state.SB651-SSA1,4,2424(3) Department duties. The department shall do all of the following:SB651-SSA1,5,21(a) Review contracts and uses of technology for approval under sub. (2) (b) and 2s. 134.13 (2) (b).SB651-SSA1,5,173(b) Establish and maintain a list of network-connected information and 4communication technologies and entities prohibited under sub. (2) (a) and s. 134.13 5(2) (a). The department shall include on the list under this paragraph all entries on 6the list under section 1260H of P.L. 116-283, the entity list published by the bureau 7of industry and security of the U.S. department of commerce as described in 15 CFR 8744.16, and the covered list published by the federal communications commission’s 9public safety and homeland security bureau as described in 47 CFR 1.50002. If the 10department adds any new network-connected information and communication 11technology or entity to the list under this paragraph, the department shall 12establish the effective date upon which use of the new network-connected 13information and communication technology is prohibited, and, upon such effective 14date, no use of the new network-connected information and communication 15technology may be put into service for critical infrastructure. The effective date 16may not be more than 90 days after the date the department adds the network-17connected information and communication technology to the list.SB651-SSA1,318Section 3. 134.13 of the statutes is created to read:SB651-SSA1,5,2019134.13 Critical infrastructure regulation. (1) Definitions. In this 20section:SB651-SSA1,5,2321(a) “Covered entity” means an entity other than a state agency that 22constructs, repairs, operates, or otherwise has significant access to critical 23infrastructure.SB651-SSA1,6,11(b) “Critical infrastructure” has the meaning given in s. 16.14 (1) (a).SB651-SSA1,6,22(c) “Department” means the department of administration.SB651-SSA1,6,33(d) “Entity” has the meaning given in s. 16.14 (1) (b).SB651-SSA1,6,44(e) “Foreign principal” has the meaning given in s. 16.14 (1) (d).SB651-SSA1,6,55(f) “Public utility” has the meaning given in s. 196.01 (5).SB651-SSA1,6,66(g) “State agency” has the meaning given in s. 16.14 (1) (e).SB651-SSA1,6,87(2) Prohibited contracts and uses of technology. (a) Except as provided 8under par. (b), no covered entity may do any of the following:SB651-SSA1,6,1191. Knowingly enter into a contract with a foreign principal that allows the 10foreign principal to directly or remotely access or control critical infrastructure in 11this state.SB651-SSA1,6,15122. Knowingly put into use in critical infrastructure any network-connected 13information and communication technology that allows a foreign principal to 14directly or remotely access or control critical infrastructure in this state and that 15appears on the list maintained by the department under s. 16.14 (3) (b).SB651-SSA1,6,2016(b) 1. Except as provided in subd. 2., if a covered entity is unable to source an 17alternative to a technology or entity on the list maintained by the department 18under s. 16.14 (3) (b), the covered entity shall certify to the department each 19contract or use of a technology described in par. (a) 1. or 2. related to critical 20infrastructure within this state.
