Referred to Committee on Natural Resources, Veteran and Military Affairs.SB1089,1,51An Act to create 20.370 (9) (pq) and 23.199 of the statutes; relating to: 2creating a Mississippi River erosion control revolving loan program, extending 3the time limit for emergency rule procedures, providing an exemption from 4emergency rule procedures, granting rule-making authority, and making an 5appropriation.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill requires the Department of Natural Resources to administer a revolving loan program to assist municipalities and owners of homes located on the shore of the Mississippi River where the structural integrity of municipal buildings or homes is threatened by erosion of the shoreline. The bill appropriates $2,000,000 to DNR for this purpose and increases DNR’s position authorization by 0.5 FTE position to administer the program. The bill requires DNR to promulgate rules to administer the program, including eligibility requirements and income limitations, and authorizes DNR to promulgate emergency rules for the period before permanent rules take effect.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB1089,11Section 1. 20.005 (3) (schedule) of the statutes: at the appropriate place, 2insert the following amounts for the purposes indicated: - See PDF for table SB1089,28Section 2. 20.370 (9) (pq) of the statutes is created to read:SB1089,2,12920.370 (9) (pq) Mississippi River erosion control revolving loan program. As a 10continuing appropriation, from the environmental fund, the amounts in the 11schedule for the Mississippi River erosion control revolving loan program under s. 1223.199.SB1089,313Section 3. 23.199 of the statutes is created to read:SB1089,2,181423.199 Mississippi River erosion control revolving loan program. (1) 15The department shall administer a revolving loan program to assist municipalities 16and owners of homes located on the shore of the Mississippi River where the 17structural integrity of municipal buildings or homes is threatened by erosion of the 18shoreline.SB1089,2,2019(2) The department shall make loans under this section from the 20appropriation account under s. 20.370 (9) (pq).SB1089,3,221(3) The department shall promulgate rules to administer this section, 1including rules establishing eligibility criteria and income limitations for loans 2under this section.SB1089,43Section 4. Nonstatutory provisions.SB1089,3,74(1) Position authorization. The authorized FTE positions for the 5department of natural resources, funded from the appropriation under s. 20.370 (9) 6(pq), are increased by 0.5 SEG position for the purpose of administering the 7Mississippi River erosion control revolving loan program.SB1089,3,198(2) Emergency rules. The department of natural resources may use the 9procedure under s. 227.24 to promulgate emergency rules under s. 23.199 for the 10period before the date on which permanent rules under s. 23.199 take effect. 11Notwithstanding s. 227.24 (1) (c) and (2), emergency rules promulgated under this 12subsection remain in effect until the first day of the 25th month beginning after the 13effective date of the emergency rules, the date on which the permanent rules take 14effect, or the effective date of the repeal of the emergency rules, whichever is earlier. 15Notwithstanding s. 227.24 (1) (a) and (3), the department of natural resources is not 16required to provide evidence that promulgating a rule under this subsection as an 17emergency rule is necessary for the preservation of public peace, health, safety, or 18welfare and is not required to provide a finding of emergency for a rule promulgated 19under this subsection.SB1089,520Section 5.
