Referred to Committee on Natural Resources, Veteran and Military Affairs.SB628,1,31An Act to create 283.90 of the statutes; relating to: notifying counties and 2tribes of an exceedance of groundwater protection standards or standards for 3PFAS.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauUnder this bill, if the Department of Natural Resources finds that there has been an exceedance of groundwater standards or of any standard for perfluoroalkyl or polyfluoroalkyl substance (commonly known as PFAS) in a proposed or promulgated DNR rule, DNR must notify the county health department, tribal health department, and county land and conservation department in the affected county. DNR must also notify the county health department, tribal health department, and county land and conservation department in any adjacent county that DNR determines may be negatively affected by the exceedance. The bill requires DNR to provide these notices within seven business days after verifying that an exceedance has occurred. The bill also requires DNR to create and maintain a notification system for notifying county health departments, tribal health departments, and county land and conservation departments of the types of exceedances that trigger the notice requirement.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB628,11Section 1. 283.90 of the statutes is created to read:SB628,2,122283.90 Notification of exceedances. (1) Whenever on the basis of any 3information available to it the department finds that there has been a exceedance of 4any groundwater protection standards under ch. 160 or of any standard for PFAS, 5as defined in s. 299.48 (1) (b), in a proposed or promulgated rule of the department, 6the department shall notify the county health department, tribal health 7department, and county land and conservation department in the affected county 8and the county health department, tribal health department, and county land and 9conservation department in any adjacent county that the department determines 10may be negatively affected as a result of the exceedance. The department shall 11provide notice within 7 business days after verifying that an exceedance has 12occurred. The notice is subject to public inspection and copying under s. 19.35 (1).SB628,2,1513(2) The department shall create and maintain a notification system for 14notifying county health departments, tribal health departments, and county land 15and conservation departments of the exceedances described in this section.SB628,2,1716(3) The department may establish procedures for providing notice under this 17section.SB628,218Section 2.
