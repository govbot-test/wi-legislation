(intro.), 814.68 (1) (a), 814.68 (2), 814.70 (1), 814.70 (2), 814.70 (3) (a), 814.70 12(3) (b), 814.70 (4) (a), 814.70 (4) (b), 814.70 (7), 814.70 (8), 814.70 (9) (a) 1(intro.), 814.70 (9) (b), 814.70 (12), 814.72, 814.85 (1) (a), 814.85 (1) (b), 814.85 2(1) (c), 814.85 (2), 814.86 (1), 814.86 (1m), 814.86 (2), 978.13 (1) (b), 978.13 (1) 3(c) and 978.13 (1) (d); to repeal and recreate 807.14; to create 814.54, 4814.59, 814.595, 814.85 (3) and 814.86 (1s) of the statutes; relating to: 5increasing certain court fees and surcharges and requiring an interpreter in a 6civil proceeding to act remotely.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill 1) increases various court fees and surcharges collected by clerks of court, municipal judges, and registers in probate, 2) increases certain court fees paid to witnesses, interpreters, supplemental court commissioners, court reporters, sheriffs, and appraisers, and 3) expands the staff in the Milwaukee County District Attorney’s office for which reimbursement for salary and fringe benefits is provided by the state. The bill also provides that, unless otherwise required by law, all court fees and surcharges retained for use by a county must be used to support court services in that county. For mileage reimbursement rates that are increased under the bill, the bill sets those rates at the rate determined by the federal Internal Revenue Service for the business standard mileage rate for federal income tax purposes.Under current law, a county must submit a portion of each amount the county collects, as specified in current law, to the Department of Administration for various state uses, and the county may retain the balance for use by the county. A similar requirement generally applies to municipalities in municipal court actions. The bill increases the amount that the county must submit to DOA for certain fees by 5 percent, but generally provides for the county to retain for use by the county the remainder of the additional amounts collected as a result of the increases in the bill.The bill also requires that each clerk of court post a notice that an application for waiver of any requirement to give security for costs or for payment for any service or fee based on a finding that a person is unable to pay is available for indigent parties in the clerk of court’s office.Under the bill, beginning in 2032, and in every third fiscal biennium thereafter, the director of state courts must, no later than the last day for agencies to submit their budget requests to DOA and the Legislative Fiscal Bureau, which is September 15 of each even-numbered year, submit to DOA, LFB, the governor, and the legislature a report that includes a calculation of the amounts of certain court fees and surcharges specified in the bill that are collected by clerks of court, municipal judges, and registers in probate, and of certain court fees paid to witnesses, interpreters, and others, in present-day dollars if the fee or surcharge were adjusted to account for inflation based on the consumer price index, rounded up to the nearest dollar.Current law provides that, at the request of any party, a court may permit an interpreter to act in any civil proceeding, other than a trial, by telephone or live audiovisual means. The bill requires an interpreter to act in a civil proceeding, including a trial, by telephone or live audiovisual means, except that if any party objects to the interpreter acting by telephone or live audiovisual means, the court must decide whether the interpreter must act in person. Also, if the interpreter is employed by the county, the interpreter must act in person. Under the bill, absent an objection, a court may permit an interpreter to act by telephone or live audiovisual means in a civil proceeding under the state children’s code, mental health act, or protective service system or in a civil proceeding regarding a guardianship or conservatorship.Under the bill, prior to any civil proceeding, a court must inform the parties that they may object to an interpreter acting by telephone or live audiovisual means. Finally, in the event of a temporary technical problem in the videoconferencing technology used by the court, an interpreter must act in person.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB333-SSA2,11Section 1. 20.475 (1) (i) of the statutes is amended to read:SB333-SSA2,3,9220.475 (1) (i) Other employees.
