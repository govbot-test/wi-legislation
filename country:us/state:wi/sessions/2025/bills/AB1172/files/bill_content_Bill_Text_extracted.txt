Referred to Committee on Sporting Heritage.AB1172,1,41An Act to amend 29.563 (3) (a) 2., 29.563 (6m) (a) and 29.563 (14) (c) 7.; to 2create 20.855 (4) (eg) and 25.29 (1) (g) of the statutes; relating to: 3eliminating the fee for an annual fishing license issued to a resident senior 4citizen and making an appropriation.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill eliminates the fee and issuing fee for an annual fishing license issued to a resident who has attained the age of 65 years (resident senior citizen). Under current law, the fee for such a license is $6.25 and the issuing fee is $0.75. The bill requires in each fiscal year a transfer from the general fund to the conservation fund in the amount of the number of annual fishing licenses issued to resident senior citizens in that fiscal year multiplied by $7.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB1172,15Section 1. 20.855 (4) (eg) of the statutes is created to read:AB1172,2,6120.855 (4) (eg) Transfer to conservation fund; resident senior citizen annual 2fishing license fee reimbursement. A sum sufficient for transfer to the conservation 3fund in an amount equal to the amount to be paid into the conservation fund as 4determined under s. 25.29 (1) (g). The amounts may be paid at such intervals 5during each fiscal year as the secretary of administration deems appropriate or 6necessary.AB1172,27Section 2. 25.29 (1) (g) of the statutes is created to read:AB1172,2,10825.29 (1) (g) Each fiscal year, an amount equal to the number of annual 9fishing licenses issued to resident senior citizens under ch. 29 in that fiscal year 10multiplied by $7.AB1172,311Section 3. 29.563 (3) (a) 2. of the statutes is amended to read:AB1172,2,121229.563 (3) (a) 2.
