Referred to Committee on Licensing, Regulatory Reform, State and Federal Affairs.SB779,1,31An Act to create 97.303 of the statutes; relating to: prohibiting food and 2beverages from being served in foam polystyrene packaging and providing a 3penalty.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill prohibits an operator of a retail food business from serving food and beverages in foam polystyrene packaging. The prohibition under the bill does not apply to either of the following: 1) containers enclosing food or beverage that have been filled and sealed outside the state; or 2) containers used for packaging raw meat, poultry, fish, or seafood to be consumed off the premises where sold. Under the bill, the Department of Agriculture, Trade and Consumer Protection may grant a waiver from the prohibition lasting up to one year after the bill takes effect to a retail food business operator. DATCP may grant a waiver under the bill if doing so is necessary to avoid an undue hardship or practical difficulty for a retail food business operator that is not common among similarly situated retail food business operators.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB779,11Section 1. 97.303 of the statutes is created to read:SB779,2,3297.303 Serving food and beverages in foam polystyrene packaging. 3(1) Definitions. In this section:SB779,2,54(a) “Foam polystyrene packaging” means packaging made primarily from 5foam polystyrene that is designed for serving or containing food or beverage.SB779,2,76(b) “Retail food business operator” means a person who operates a retail food 7establishment, as defined in s. 97.30 (1) (c), or a vending machine operator.SB779,2,98(2) Prohibition. (a) Except as provided in par. (b), no retail food business 9operator may serve food or beverages in foam polystyrene packaging.SB779,2,1010(b) Paragraph (a) does not apply to any of the following:SB779,2,12111. A container enclosing food or beverage that has been filled and sealed 12outside this state before receipt by a retail food business operator.SB779,2,14132. A container used for packaging raw meat, poultry, fish, or seafood to be 14consumed off the premises where sold.SB779,2,2215(3) Waiver. Notwithstanding sub. (2) (a), the department may grant to a 16retail food business operator a waiver authorizing activity prohibited under sub. (2) 17(a) if the department determines that the waiver is necessary to avoid an undue 18hardship or practical difficulty for the retail food business operator that is not 19common among similarly situated retail food business operators. A waiver granted 20under this subsection may not authorize activity prohibited under sub. (2) (a) that 21occurs on or after the first day of the 13th month beginning after the effective date 22of this subsection .... [LRB inserts date].SB779,3,51(4) Penalties. A person who violates sub.
