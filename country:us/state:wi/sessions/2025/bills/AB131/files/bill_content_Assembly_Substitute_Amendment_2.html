AB131-ASA2,5,224281.61 (6) Priority list. The department shall establish a priority list that
5ranks each safe drinking water loan program project. The department shall
6promulgate rules for determining project rankings that, to the extent possible, give
7priority to projects that address the most serious risks to human health, that are
8necessary to ensure compliance with the Safe Drinking Water Act, 42 USC 300f to
9300j-26, and that assist applicants that are most in need on a per household basis,
10according to affordability criteria specified in the rules. For the purpose of ranking
11projects under this subsection, the department shall treat a project to upgrade a
12public water system to provide continuous disinfection of the water that it
13distributes as if the public water system were a surface water system that federal
14law requires to provide continuous disinfection. If the department, when ranking
15projects under this subsection or determining an applicant’s eligibility for
16assistance under this section, considers whether an applicant that intends to
17extend service outside the boundaries of a local governmental unit because of water
18contamination is small or disadvantaged, the department shall, to the extent
19allowable under federal law, determine the applicant to be small or disadvantaged
20if the area receiving the extended service would normally be determined to be small
21or disadvantaged, regardless of whether the existing service area would normally
22be determined to be small or disadvantaged.
