Referred to Committee on Government Operations, Labor and Economic Development.SB1017,1,21An Act to create 20.437 (2) (d) of the statutes; relating to: funding for child 2care provider workforce training and making an appropriation.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauCurrent law authorizes the Department of Children and Families to contract with one or more agencies for the provision of training and technical assistance to improve the quality of child care provided in this state. Under current law, these contracts are funded from an allocation of federal moneys, including child care development funds and moneys received under the Temporary Assistance for Needy Families block grant program. This bill appropriates $2,166,600 in general purpose revenue funding in each fiscal year of the 2025-27 fiscal biennium for contracts that focus on workforce training for child care providers and prospective providers.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB1017,13Section 1. 20.005 (3) (schedule) of the statutes: at the appropriate place, 4insert the following amounts for the purposes indicated: - See PDF for table SB1017,26Section 2. 20.437 (2) (d) of the statutes is created to read:SB1017,2,10720.437 (2) (d) Child care training and technical assistance.
