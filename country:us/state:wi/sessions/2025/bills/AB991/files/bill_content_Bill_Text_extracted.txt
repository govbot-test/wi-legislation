Referred to Committee on State Affairs.AB991,1,21An Act to create 103.66 (1) (c) of the statutes; relating to: allowing 2employment of minors in the operation of a pizza-dough roller.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauCurrent law allows the Department of Workforce Development to set minimum ages for hazardous employment for minors. Under that authority, DWD has, by rule, prohibited all minors from being employed in certain duties related to the operation of power-driven bakery machines. This bill requires DWD to allow minors 16 years of age or older to operate a pizza-dough roller in the course of employment if certain safety standards are met and the minor does not set up, adjust, repair, oil, or clean the pizza-dough roller.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB991,13Section 1. 103.66 (1) (c) of the statutes is created to read:AB991,2,24103.66 (1) (c) Permit the employment of a minor 16 years of age or over to 1operate a pizza-dough roller in the course of employment if the safeguards described 2in 29 CFR 570.62 (b) (2) are met and the following conditions apply:AB991,2,431. The design of the pizza-dough roller prevents fingers, hands, and clothing 4from being caught in the pizza-dough rollers.AB991,2,552. The gears of the pizza-dough roller are completely enclosed.AB991,2,763. The machine has microswitches that disengage the machinery if the backs 7or sides of the pizza-dough rollers are removed.AB991,2,984. All safeguards described in subds. 1. to 3. are present on the machine and 9operational and have not been overridden.AB991,2,11105.
