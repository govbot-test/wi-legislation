7that chapter of the code takes effect as provided in s. 227.22, unless the rule
8provides for an earlier repeal date, or on the January 1 of the 7th year after the year

13schedule established under subd. 2. unless it is repealed earlier.

16subdivision .... [LRB inserts date], establish a schedule for the expiration of all

18code chapters on any January 1 beginning with January 1, 2028, and ending with
19January 1, 2035. The joint committee for review of administrative rules shall have
20the schedule published on the legislature’s website and shall provide a copy of the
21schedule to each affected agency.

SB277-ASA3,5,153(2) Readoption of chapter by notice. (a) No earlier than January 1 and no
4later than March 1 of the year preceding the expiration date of a chapter of the code,
5an agency may submit to the chief clerk of each house of the legislature and to the
6joint committee for review of administrative rules a notice that it intends to readopt
7the expiring chapter. The notice shall be submitted in triplicate. An agency may
8not propose to readopt more than one chapter per notice. The presiding officer of
9each house of the legislature shall, within 10 working days after the date on which
10the notice is received, direct the appropriate chief clerk to refer the notice to one
11standing committee and to enter a statement that a proposed code chapter
12readoption notice has been received in the journal of his or her house. The agency
13shall submit a copy of the notice, in an electronic format approved by the legislative
14reference bureau, to the legislative council staff and to the legislative reference
15bureau for publication in the register. The notice shall contain all of the following:
SB277-ASA3,5,16161. A brief statement explaining the basis and purpose of the chapter.
SB277-ASA3,5,22172. A reference to each statute that is interpreted by any rules contained in the
18chapter, a reference to each statute that explicitly authorizes the promulgation of
19each section contained in that chapter, and an explanation of the agency’s explicit
20authority to promulgate the rules contained in the chapter under those statutes.
21Sections 227.10 and 227.11 may not be cited as explicit authority to promulgate any
22portions of the chapter.
SB277-ASA3,6,2233. A statement affirming that all rules contained in the chapter are in
1compliance with the requirements under this subchapter and are not otherwise in
2need of revision due to changes to state or federal law or for any other reason.
SB277-ASA3,6,534. A statement affirming that the chapter is being readopted without any
4changes in substance or form and that the readopted chapter is identical to the
5chapter currently published in the administrative code.
SB277-ASA3,6,765. An analysis of any redundancy of the rules contained in the chapter with
7federal law.
SB277-ASA3,6,118(b) 1. Upon receipt of a readoption notice under par. (a), the chairperson or
9cochairpersons of each standing committee and the joint committee for review of
10administrative rules shall provide a copy of the notice to each other member of the
11committee.

14certification to the standing committees and joint committee for review of

16requirements under par. (a) 1. to 5.
SB277-ASA3,6,2417(c) The review period for each standing committee and the joint committee for
18review of administrative rules extends for 40 days after the agency submits the
19readoption notice under par. (a). During the review period, each committee to
20which the notice is referred shall review the proposed readoption and may hold a
21public hearing to review a proposed readoption or request that the agency meet
22with the committee to review the proposed readoption. A standing committee may
23meet separately or jointly with the other standing committee to which the notice
24under par. (a) was referred.
