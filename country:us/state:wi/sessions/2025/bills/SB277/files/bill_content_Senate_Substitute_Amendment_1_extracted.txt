relating to: 5the expiration of administrative rules.Analysis by the Legislative Reference Bureau2025 Assembly Bill 274 bill provides for the periodic expiration of each chapter of the administrative code and establishes a process for an agency to readopt a chapter by submitting a readoption notice for review by one standing committee in each house of the legislature and the Joint Committee for Review of Administrative Rules (JCRAR). Under the bill, any member of a standing committee or JCRAR may then object to the readoption of the chapter, in which case the agency may only readopt the chapter by using the rule-making process. This substitute amendment differs from 2025 Assembly Bill 274 in the following respects:1. The substitute amendment requires an agency, when seeking to readopt a chapter of the administrative code, to certify that it is readopting the chapter without any changes in substance or form and that the readopted chapter is identical to the chapter currently published in the administrative register.2. Current law, subject to numerous exceptions and limitations, 1) provides for general rule-making authority of each agency and 2) requires an agency to promulgate as a rule each statement of general policy and each interpretation of a statute which it specifically adopts to govern its enforcement or administration of that statute. The substitute amendment requires an agency, when seeking to readopt a chapter of the administrative code, to cite its “explicit” authority for promulgating the rules in that chapter and provides that an agency may not, when seeking to readopt a rule, cite these general grants of rule-making authority as the agency’s explicit authority to promulgate the rules in that chapter.3. With respect to review of readoption notices by legislative standing committees and JCRAR, the substitute amendment maintains the review periods for the committees and provides that, during a review period, each committee must review a proposed readoption and may hold a public hearing to review a proposed readoption or request that the agency meet with the committee to review the proposed readoption. However, the substitute amendment removes the ability of a member of a committee to object to the readoption of a code chapter and instead requires the Legislative Council Staff to make a certification to the standing committees and JCRAR as to whether an agency’s readoption notice complies with requirements in the substitute amendment. The substitute amendment provides that if the Legislative Council Staff concludes that an agency’s certification does not satisfy the requirements in the substitute amendment, the chapter may not be readopted unless the agency corrects the identified deficiencies or promulgates a rule to readopt the chapter.4. Under the bill, JCRAR may, at any time prior to the expiration date of a chapter, extend the expiration date for the chapter for a period specified by JCRAR not to exceed one year. The substitute amendment allows JCRAR to grant an additional extension of up to one year if the chapter pertains to the issuance of permits, licenses, certifications, or regulatory approvals or is necessary to remain in compliance with federal law.5. The substitute amendment provides that if an agency readopts an expiring chapter of the administrative code using the rule-making process, the rule is subject to certain limitations, including that the readoption rule may not result in any new or additional implementation or compliance costs for any business, local government, or individual subject to the rule and may not create a new or more stringent standard, requirement, or threshold.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB277-SSA1,11Section 1. 35.93 (2) (b) 3. gn. of the statutes is created to read:SB277-SSA1,3,2235.93 (2) (b) 3. gn.
