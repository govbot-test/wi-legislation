The credit is equal to 30 percent of the eligible wages paid by the claimant directly relating to developing, producing, or creating a video game product. Under the bill, “video game product” means, subject to certain exceptions, an electronic game developed for commercial distribution and entertainment that involves interaction with a used interface to generate visual feedback on a video device. “Video game product” does not include social media, gambling products, or products with obscene content.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB204-SSA2,11Section 1. 71.05 (6) (a) 15. of the statutes, as affected by 2025 Wisconsin Act 215, is amended to read:SB204-SSA2,2,8371.05 (6) (a) 15. The amount of the credits computed under s. 71.07 (2dm), 4(2dx), (2dy), (3g), (3h), (3n), (3q), (3s), (3t), (3w), (3wm), (3y), (4k), (4n), (5f), (5h), 5(5i), (5j), (5k), (5r), (5rm), (6n), and (10), and (12) and not passed through by a 6partnership, limited liability company, or tax-option corporation that has added 7that amount to the partnership’s, company’s, or tax-option corporation’s income 8under s. 71.21 (4) or 71.34 (1k) (g).SB204-SSA2,29Section 2. 71.07 (12) of the statutes is created to read:SB204-SSA2,2,111071.07 (12) Video game production credit. (a) Definitions. In this 11subsection:SB204-SSA2,2,12121. “Claimant” means a person who files a claim under this subsection.SB204-SSA2,2,14132. “Eligible wages” means wages paid by the claimant directly relating to 14qualifying activities in this state.SB204-SSA2,2,16153. “Qualifying activities” means developing, producing, or creating a video 16game product or platform.SB204-SSA2,2,20174. “Video game product or platform” means an electronic game developed for 18commercial distribution and entertainment that involves interaction with a user 19interface to generate visual feedback on a video device. “Video game product or 20platform” does not include any of the following:SB204-SSA2,2,2121a. A product or platform that contains content in violation of s. 944.21.SB204-SSA2,2,2222b. A product or platform designed for political or religious purposes.SB204-SSA2,2,2323c. A product or platform regulated under chs. 562 to 569.SB204-SSA2,3,21d. A product or platform intended for use by gambling machine, as defined in 2s. 945.01 (3).SB204-SSA2,3,53e. A product or platform designed to provide information about one or more 4individuals or businesses, such as marketing and promotional websites or 5microsites.SB204-SSA2,3,96f. A product or platform designed for social media or interpersonal 7communications, such as podcasting, interactive advertising, journalism, video 8conferencing, wireless telecommunications, text-based channels, chat rooms, or 9broadcast or streamed viewing enhancements.SB204-SSA2,3,1110g. A product or platform designed to stream linear media such as audio, video, 11or pictures.SB204-SSA2,3,1212h. Small-scale electronic games embedded and used exclusively in advertising.SB204-SSA2,3,15135. “Wages” means wages, as defined in s. 71.63 (6), that are subject to 14withholding under subch. X and for which a statement under s. 71.65 (1) is 15furnished.SB204-SSA2,3,1916(b) Filing claims. Subject to the limitations provided in this subsection, for 17taxable years beginning after December 31, 2025, a claimant may claim as a credit 18against the tax imposed under s. 71.02, up to the amount of those taxes, an amount 19equal to 30 percent of the eligible wages paid by the person in the taxable year.SB204-SSA2,4,420(c) Limitations.
