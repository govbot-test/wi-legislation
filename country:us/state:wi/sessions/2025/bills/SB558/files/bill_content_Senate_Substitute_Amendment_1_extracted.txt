Such expenses include fixing or replacing personal property damaged by the disaster, home repair that is conducted by a dwelling contractor certified by the Department of Safety and Professional Services and that is necessary due to damage from the disaster, and temporary housing assistance.Also under the bill, a grant of not more than $50,000 may be awarded to a business for the eligible disaster-related expenses of fixing or replacing the business’s property damaged by the disaster, repair to the business necessary due to damage from the disaster, payroll assistance, working capital, and other normal business operating expenses. “Business” is defined in the bill to mean an organization conducting business in this state, including credit unions but not including other nonprofit organizations organized as nonstock corporations.Under the bill, an individual may not receive a grant if a business that operates at the individual’s residence or contiguously to the individual’s residence has been awarded a grant for repairs to the same residence. Conversely, a business that operates within an individual’s residence or contiguously to an individual’s residence may not receive a grant if the individual has been awarded a grant for repairs to the same residence.Under the bill, individuals and businesses must provide to DMA a receipt or other documentation, as determined by DMA, for any disaster-related eligible expenses for which they seek a grant and sign and provide to DMA an affidavit committing to return to DMA any part of a grant received that is duplicated by the receipt of insurance money or other source of disaster-related assistance that is not a loan awarded directly by the federal government. An individual must apply for the grant in the manner determined by DMA by the later of the following: 1) within 90 days after a determination is made by the Federal Emergency Management Agency to not provide aid to the individual for the disaster-related expenses, if applicable; 2) within 90 days after the governor declares an emergency if the disaster-related damages do not meet the minimum qualifications for individual federal aid; or 3) within 90 days after the date on which the bill becomes law. A business must apply for the grant in the manner determined by DMA within 90 days after the governor declares an emergency or within 90 days after the date on which the bill becomes law, whichever is later.Additionally, no grants for individuals or businesses may be awarded if the president of the United States has declared a major disaster to exist in this state and federal aid is made available to individuals and businesses in a manner similar to the grant program under the bill. DMA must process applications for grants and award to qualified individuals and businesses a grant within 30 days of the individual’s or business’s application for the grant. However, DMA is not required to make a grant if it does not have sufficient funds available to do so.The bill creates 2.0 project positions to establish the program under the bill and 1.0 FTE position to administer the program under the bill. The bill allows DMA to request supplemental funding and additional temporary FTE positions from the Joint Committee on Finance in any year in which DMA estimates or concludes that it needs additional funding in order to award grants relating to all eligible requests it receives. If JCF receives such a request, it must hold a meeting as soon as practical after receiving the request to consider the request. Additionally, under the bill, DMA may submit a request for approval to JCF under passive review to transfer any amount appropriated for a grant program under the bill for use for another grant program under the bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB558-SSA1,11Section 1. 13.101 (18) of the statutes is created to read:SB558-SSA1,3,5113.101 (18) If the department of military affairs requests that the joint 2committee on finance supplement an appropriation account under s. 323.32 (5) (c) 31. or create, on a temporary basis, a portion of a position, as defined in s. 230.03 4(11), under s. 323.32 (5) (c) 2., the joint committee on finance shall hold a meeting as 5soon as practicable after receiving the request to consider the request.SB558-SSA1,26Section 2. 323.32 of the statutes is created to read:SB558-SSA1,3,87323.32 Disaster assistance for individuals and businesses grant 8programs.
