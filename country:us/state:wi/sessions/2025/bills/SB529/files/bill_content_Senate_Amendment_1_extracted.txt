No modification may be claimed under this subdivision for 7an amount paid for tuition expenses and mandatory student fees, as described 8under this subdivision, if the source of the payment is an amount withdrawn from a 9college savings account, as described in s. 224.50 or from a college tuition and 10expenses program, as described in s. 224.48, and if the owner of the account or a 11parent, grandparent, great-grandparent, aunt, or uncle of the beneficiary another 1individual, who contributed to the account, has claimed a deduction under subd. 32. 2or 33. that relates to such an amount.”.SB529-SA1,2,433. Except as otherwise provided in this subdivision, an 7amount equal to not more than $5,000 per beneficiary, by each contributor, or 8$2,500 by each contributor who is married and files separately, to an account for 9each year to which the claim relates, except that the total amount for which a 10deduction may be claimed under this subdivision and under subd. 33., per 11beneficiary by any claimant may not exceed $5,000 each year, or $2,500 each year by 12any claimant who is married and files separately. In the case of a married couple, 13the total deduction under this subdivision and under subd. 33., per beneficiary by 14the married couple may not exceed $5,000 each year. For taxable years beginning 15after December 31, 2024, the dollar amounts in this subd. 32. a., and the dollar 16amounts in subd. 33. a., shall be increased each year by a percentage equal to the 17percentage change between the U.S. consumer price index for all urban consumers, 18U.S. city average, for the month of August of the previous year and the U.S. 19consumer price index for all urban consumers, U.S. city average, for the month of 20August 2023, as determined by the federal department of labor, except that the 21adjustment may occur only if the resulting amount is greater than the 22corresponding amount that was calculated for the previous year. Each amount that 23is revised under this subd. 32. a. and under subd. 33. a. shall be rounded to the 24nearest multiple of $10 if the revised amount is not a multiple of $10 or, if the 1revised amount is a multiple of $5, such an amount shall be increased to the next 2higher multiple of $10. The department of revenue shall annually adjust the 3changes in dollar amounts required under this subd. 32. a. and incorporate the 4changes into the income tax forms and instructions. Any amount that is paid into 5an account under this subdivision that exceeds the maximum amount that may be 6subtracted under this subdivision may be carried forward to the next taxable year, 7and thereafter, subject to the limitations in this subdivision.”.SB529-SA1,3,984. Page 27, line 20: delete the material beginning with that line and ending 9on page 28, line 12.SB529-SA1,3,10105. For purposes of the apportionment of any 13income under this section, the disaster relief work, as defined in s. 323.12 (5) (a) 3., 14of an out-of-state business, as defined in s. 323.12 (5) (a) 6., shall not increase the 15amount of income apportioned to this state. For purposes of sub. (7), any property 16brought temporarily into this state by an out-of-state business in connection with 17performing disaster relief work is not considered property located in this state. For 18purposes of sub. (8), compensation paid to out-of-state employees, as defined in s. 19323.12 (5) (a) 7., who are performing disaster relief work is not considered 20compensation paid in this state. For purposes of sub. (9), gross receipts from the 21sale of property or services as part of performing any disaster relief work are not 22considered gross receipts from sales received in this state.”.SB529-SA1,3,23236.
