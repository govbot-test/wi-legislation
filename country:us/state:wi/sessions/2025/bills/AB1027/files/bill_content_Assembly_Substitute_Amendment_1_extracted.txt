2to create 49.79 (13) and 49.79 (14) of the statutes; relating to: requiring 3information relating to the food stamp program to be compiled and provided to 4the U.S. Department of Agriculture and food stamp program work 5requirements, eligibility requirements, and administration.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauUnder current law, the federal food stamp program, known as the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) and called FoodShare in this state, provides benefits to eligible low-income households for the purchase of food. The food stamp program is administered by the Department of Health Services. Under current law, the federal government pays the benefits for the food stamp program while the state and federal government share the cost of administration.Under current law, able-bodied adults without dependents (ABAWD) must fulfill certain work requirements in order to have continued eligibility for the food stamp program. This bill modifies the applicability of these work requirements to conform to changes in federal law passed as part of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act of 2025 (OBBBA). First, the bill changes the definition of “able-bodied adult” by increasing the upper age limit exception from age 50 to age 65. The bill also narrows, consistent with federal law, the work requirement exemption for caregivers and parents by changing the definition of ABAWD to exclude able-bodied adults who are parents of or reside with a household member who is under 14 years of age rather than a household member who is under 18 years of age.The bill provides that only individuals who meet the federal definitions of an eligible alien and qualified alien are eligible to receive benefits under the food stamp program. Under the bill, DHS must verify eligibility during enrollment and eligibility recertification using the Systematic Alien Verification for Entitlements online service or by requiring the individual to provide an acceptable form of proof of citizenship or eligible alien status.Under the bill, DHS must provide to the federal Department of Agriculture information regarding enrollment in the food stamp program, including the identity of individuals receiving benefits. Under the bill, DHS must provide this information at the frequency and in the format required by the federal Food and Nutrition Service of the Department of Agriculture. The bill also provides that, within six months after the bill becomes law, DHS must compile and share with the federal Food and Nutrition Service of the Department of Agriculture all data required by the federal Department of Agriculture as set forth in that department’s letter to state agency directors dated July 23, 2025, regarding individuals who, since January 1, 2020, have received, are currently receiving, or have applied to receive benefits under the federal food stamp program.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB1027-ASA1,11Section 1. 49.79 (1) (am) 2. of the statutes is amended to read:AB1027-ASA1,2,2249.79 (1) (am) 2.
