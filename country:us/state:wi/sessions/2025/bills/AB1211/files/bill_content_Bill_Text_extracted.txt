Referred to Committee on Energy and Utilities.AB1211,1,31An Act to create 20.155 (3) (c) and 197.11 of the statutes; relating to: grants 2to municipalities to acquire or create public utilities and making an 3appropriation.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauUnder current law, a municipality has the power to construct and operate a plant and equipment for the production, transmission, delivery, or furnishing of heat, light, water, or power; to purchase any public utility on terms approved by the Public Service Commission; and to acquire the property of any public utility. “Municipality” is defined to mean any town, village, or city wherein property of a public utility or any part thereof is located. This bill creates a grant program under which PSC must award a grant to any municipality that takes any of the aforementioned actions for the purpose of owning and operating a public utility if the municipality has secured from PSC a certificate of public convenience and necessity to do so. Under the bill, such a grant must cover 50 percent of the costs of construction or acquisition. The bill funds the grant program with a sum sufficient appropriation.For further information see the state and local fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB1211,11Section 1. 20.155 (3) (c) of the statutes is created to read:AB1211,2,3220.155 (3) (c) Municipal public utility grants. A sum sufficient for the 3municipal public utility grants awarded under s. 197.11.AB1211,24Section 2. 197.11 of the statutes is created to read:AB1211,2,155197.11 Municipal public utility grants. (1) The commission shall award 6a grant as provided under this section to any municipality in this state that, for the 7purpose of owning and operating a public utility, seeks to construct and operate a 8plant or equipment under s. 197.01 (1), to purchase a public utility or any part 9thereof under s. 197.01 (2), or to acquire the property of any public utility under s. 10197.01 (3) and has secured from the commission a certificate of public convenience 11and necessity to construct, own, operate, manage, or control any plant or equipment 12for the production, transmission, delivery, or furnishing of heat, light, water, or 13power. A municipality may apply to the commission for a grant under this section 14in a manner and form determined by the commission and shall submit with the 15application all information requested by the commission.AB1211,2,1716(2) The commission shall award a grant under this section in the amount of 1750 percent of the costs of the construction or acquisition under sub. (1).
