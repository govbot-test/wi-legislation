(1) Group homes, shelter care 6facilities, and child welfare agencies that operate a residential care center for 7children and youth may use video surveillance and recordings in common areas, 8entrances, and exits without the consent of the child being surveilled or recorded. 9The group home, shelter care facility, or child welfare agency shall inform the child 1and the child's parent, guardian, legal custodian, or Indian custodian about the 2video surveillance and recording.SB485-ASA1,2,63(2) All child welfare agencies that operate a residential care center for 4children and youth, all group homes, and all shelter care facilities shall adopt a 5policy for monitoring safety in those facilities, which may include the use of video 6surveillance and recording in common areas, entrances, and exits.SB485-ASA1,2,97(3) Video surveillance and recording authorized under subs. (1) and (2) may 8not be used as a substitute for one-on-one monitoring of a child who is at high risk 9for self-harm.SB485-ASA1,2,1510(4) Video recording data under this section is confidential and not open to 11public inspection, except that the exceptions to the confidentiality of records that 12apply to agencies under s. 48.78 (2) apply to residential care centers for children 13and youth, group homes, and shelter care facilities with respect to video recording 14data under this subsection. The department may review any recording made under 15this section.SB485-ASA1,216Section 2. 51.61 (1) (o) of the statutes is amended to read:SB485-ASA1,3,111751.61 (1) (o) Except as otherwise provided, have a right not to be filmed or 18taped, unless the patient signs an informed and voluntary consent that specifically 19authorizes a named individual or group to film or tape the patient for a particular 20purpose or project during a specified time period. The patient may specify in the 21consent periods during which, or situations in which, the patient may not be filmed 22or taped. If a patient is adjudicated incompetent, the consent shall be granted on 23behalf of the patient by the patient’s guardian. A patient in Goodland Hall at the 24Mendota Mental Health Institute, a patient detained or committed under ch. 980, 1or a patient who is in the legal custody of or under the supervision of the 2department of corrections, may be subject to video surveillance or filmed or taped 3without the patient’s consent, except that such a patient may not be filmed in 4patient bedrooms or bathrooms without the patient’s consent unless the patient is 5engaged in dangerous or disruptive behavior. A patient placed in a residential care 6center for children and youth, group home, or shelter care facility licensed under ch. 748 may be subject to video surveillance or filmed or taped without the patient’s 8consent as authorized under s. 48.672. A treatment activity involving a patient 9committed or detained under ch. 980 may be filmed or taped if the purpose of the 10recording is to assess the quality of the treatment activity or to facilitate clinical 11supervision of the staff involved in the treatment activity.SB485-ASA1,312Section 3. Effective date.SB485-ASA1,3,1313(1) This act takes effect 30 days after publication.SB485-ASA1,3,1414(end) Down Down /2025/related/amendments/sb485/asa1_sb485 true amends /2025/related/amendments/sb485/asa1_sb485 amends/2025/REG/SB485-ASA1 amends/2025/REG/SB485-ASA1 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Amendments»SB485»Amendment ASA1-SB485 × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
