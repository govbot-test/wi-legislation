Referred to Committee on Senate Organization.SJR142,1,11Relating to: recognizing the life and extraordinary leadership of Bob Harlan.SJR142,1,42Whereas, Robert E. “Bob” Harlan, born in Des Moines, Iowa, and a longtime 3resident of Green Bay, Wisconsin, passed away on March 5, 2026, at the age of 89; 4andSJR142,1,85Whereas, Bob was elected president and chief executive officer of the Green 6Bay Packers on June 5, 1989, and led the organization for nearly two decades until 7his retirement in January 2008, retiring as the winningest team president in NFL 8history; andSJR142,1,139Whereas, under Bob’s visionary leadership, the Packers transformed from a 10struggling franchise into one of the NFL’s most successful organizations, compiling 11the league’s best overall record of 152–88 from 1993 to 2007, achieving 13 winning 12seasons from 1992 to 2007, and returning the Lombardi Trophy to Green Bay with 13a victory in Super Bowl XXXI; andSJR142,2,414Whereas, Bob’s inspired decision to hire Ron Wolf as general manager in 1991 1and grant him full authority over football operations set the foundation for the 2franchise’s sustained success, leading to the trade for Hall of Fame quarterback 3Brett Favre, the signing of Hall of Fame defensive end Reggie White, and a 4generation of Packers victories; andSJR142,2,75Whereas, Bob spearheaded the $295 million redevelopment of historic 6Lambeau Field, completed in 2003, transforming the iconic stadium into a 7cornerstone of the franchise’s long-term financial strength; andSJR142,2,118Whereas, Bob was deeply engaged in the greater Green Bay community, 9serving on numerous boards and donating his time to charitable causes, and 10received multiple local and state honors, including an honorary degree from 11Marquette University; andSJR142,2,1312Whereas, Bob was inducted into the Green Bay Packers Hall of Fame in 2004; 13andSJR142,2,1614Whereas, Bob is survived by his beloved wife, Madeline, and his sons, Kevin, 15Bryan, and Michael, and their families, who carry on his legacy of devotion to 16family, community, and the Green Bay Packers;
