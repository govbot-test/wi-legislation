to create 1.12 (5) (c), 11.0101 (14m), 11.0101 2(26m), 11.0108, 11.0803 (1) (e), 11.0805, 11.1208 (4) (b), 16.14, 134.13 and 3196.95 of the statutes; relating to: prohibiting critical telecommunications 4infrastructure from including equipment manufactured by a foreign adversary 5and expressly prohibiting a foreign national from making a contribution to a 6referendum committee and providing a penalty.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB683-ASA4,17Section 1. 1.12 (5) (c) of the statutes is created to read:AB683-ASA4,2,281.12 (5) (c) It is the policy of this state that any network-connected 9information and communication technology that would allow a foreign principal, as 10defined in s. 16.14 (1) (d), to directly or remotely access or control critical 1infrastructure, as defined in s. 16.14 (1) (a), related to energy in this state should 2not be used in that critical infrastructure.AB683-ASA4,23Section 2. 11.0101 (14m) of the statutes is created to read:AB683-ASA4,2,4411.0101 (14m) “Foreign national” means any of the following:AB683-ASA4,2,65(a) An individual who is not a citizen of the United States and who is not 6lawfully admitted for permanent residence, as defined in 8 USC 1101 (a) (20).AB683-ASA4,2,77(b) A government of a foreign country.AB683-ASA4,2,88(c) A foreign political party.AB683-ASA4,2,119(d) A partnership, association, corporation, organization, or other combination 10of persons organized under the laws of or having its principal place of business in a 11foreign country.AB683-ASA4,2,1912(e) An entity, such as a partnership, association, corporation, or organization, 13having its principal place of business in the United States that is wholly or majority 14owned by any foreign national, as defined under pars. (a) to (d), unless any 15contribution or disbursement subject to this chapter made by the entity derives 16entirely from funds generated by the entity’s U.S. operations and all decisions 17concerning such contributions or disbursements are made by individuals who are 18U.S. citizens or individuals lawfully admitted for permanent residence, as defined 19in 8 USC 1101 (a) (20).AB683-ASA4,320Section 3. 11.0101 (26m) of the statutes is created to read:AB683-ASA4,2,242111.0101 (26m) “Preliminary activity” means activity occurring prior to filing 22a registration statement under s. 11.0803 and includes preparing and analyzing 23referendum language and ballot questions, conducting a poll, survey, or focus group, 24making communications, and travel.AB683-ASA4,41Section 4. 11.0108 of the statutes is created to read:AB683-ASA4,3,9211.0108 Donor privacy protection. Any investigation of an alleged 3violation of this chapter or a lawful court order in an action brought pursuant to 4this chapter shall occur in a manner that shields the identity of lawful donors to a 5tax-exempt organization as much as possible. No person, including government and 6judicial actors, may collect or require the submission of information on the identity 7of any donor to a tax-exempt organization other than what is directly related to an 8alleged violation of this chapter. Any release of donor information in violation of 9this subsection is a violation of s. 19.50 (1).AB683-ASA4,510Section 5. 11.0803 (1) (e) of the statutes is created to read:AB683-ASA4,3,121111.0803 (1) (e) An affirmation that none of the referendum committee’s 12preliminary activity was funded, directly or indirectly, by a foreign national.AB683-ASA4,613Section 6. 11.0805 of the statutes is created to read:AB683-ASA4,3,201411.0805 Foreign nationals; prohibited contributions. (1) 15Affirmations. Each referendum committee shall obtain from a person making a 16contribution, donation, or gift to the referendum committee a written affirmation 17that the person is not a foreign national and has not intentionally accepted 18contributions, donations, or gifts aggregating in excess of $100,000 from one or 19more foreign nationals during the 4-year period immediately preceding the date on 20which the person makes the contribution, donation, or gift. AB683-ASA4,4,721(2) Reports. For each report required under s. 11.0804, a referendum 22committee shall attest that it has obtained all the written affirmations under sub. 23(1) for all contributions, donations, and gifts accepted by the committee. The 24committee shall also affirm that the donor associated with each contribution, 1donation, or gift is not a foreign national, that the donor has not intentionally 2received, solicited, or accepted, directly or indirectly, contributions, donations, or 3gifts aggregating in excess of $100,000 from one or more foreign nationals during 4the 4-year period immediately preceding the date on which the committee accepts 5the contribution, donation, or gift, and that the committee has not intentionally 6received, solicited, or accepted a contribution, donation, or gift from a foreign 7national.AB683-ASA4,78Section 7. 11.1208 (4) of the statutes is renumbered 11.1208 (4) (a).AB683-ASA4,89Section 8. 11.1208 (4) (b) of the statutes is created to read:AB683-ASA4,4,131011.1208 (4) (b) 1. No foreign national may, directly or indirectly, make a 11contribution, donation, or gift to a referendum committee and no referendum 12committee may, directly or indirectly, receive, solicit, or accept a contribution, 13donation, or gift from a foreign national.AB683-ASA4,4,17142. No foreign national may direct, dictate, control, or directly or indirectly 15participate in the decision-making process of any person regarding the person’s 16activities to support or oppose a referendum or to influence a particular vote at a 17referendum.AB683-ASA4,4,20183. No foreign national may solicit, directly or indirectly, the making of a 19donation, contribution, or expenditure by another person to influence a 20referendum.AB683-ASA4,921Section 9. 16.14 of the statutes is created to read:AB683-ASA4,4,232216.14 Contracts and uses of technology related to critical 23infrastructure. (1) Definitions. In this section:AB683-ASA4,5,524(a) “Critical infrastructure” means systems and assets, whether physical or 1virtual or publicly or privately held, so vital to this state or to the United States that 2the incapacity or destruction of such systems and assets would have a debilitating 3effect on state or national security, state or national economic security, state or 4national public health, or any combination of the foregoing. “Critical 5infrastructure” includes all of the following:AB683-ASA4,5,661. Gas and oil production, storage, and delivery systems.AB683-ASA4,5,772. Water supply, refinement, storage, and delivery systems.AB683-ASA4,5,883. Telecommunications networks.AB683-ASA4,5,994. Electrical power delivery systems.AB683-ASA4,5,10105. Emergency services.AB683-ASA4,5,11116. Transportation systems and services.AB683-ASA4,5,1212(b) “Entity” means a person other than an individual.AB683-ASA4,5,1413(c) 1. Except as provided under subd. 2., “foreign adversary” means any of the 14following:AB683-ASA4,5,1615a. The People’s Republic of China, including the Hong Kong Special 16Administrative Region and the Macau Special Administrative Region.AB683-ASA4,5,1717b. The Republic of Cuba.AB683-ASA4,5,1818c. The Islamic Republic of Iran.AB683-ASA4,5,1919d. The Democratic People’s Republic of Korea.AB683-ASA4,5,2020e. The Russian Federation.AB683-ASA4,5,2121f. The Venezuelan regime of Nicolás Maduro.AB683-ASA4,6,2222. “Foreign adversary” does not include any country, government, regime, or 23person specified under subd. 1. if the country, government, regime, or person is not 1included in the list of foreign adversaries under 15 CFR 791.4, as amended from 2time to time.AB683-ASA4,6,33(d) “Foreign principal” means any of the following:AB683-ASA4,6,441. A foreign adversary or an agent of a foreign adversary.AB683-ASA4,6,552. An individual who is a citizen of a country controlled by a foreign adversary.AB683-ASA4,6,863. A business entity that is organized under the laws of a country controlled by 7a foreign adversary and whose principal place of business is located within territory 8controlled by that foreign adversary.AB683-ASA4,6,1094. An investment fund that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary or 10agent of a foreign adversary.AB683-ASA4,6,13115. An entity that has 50 percent or more of its stock, securities, or other 12indicia of ownership owned or controlled, directly or indirectly, by a foreign 13adversary or other person or combination of persons described in subds. 1. to 4.AB683-ASA4,6,19146. An entity whose governance structure or internal monitoring or human 15resources decision-making is consistent with the objectives set forth in the Opinion 16on Strengthening the United Front Work of the Private Economy in the New Era 17issued on September 15, 2020, by the General Office of the Central Committee of 18the Chinese Communist Party or a successor or similar document issued by the 19Chinese Communist Party by an agent of the Chinese Communist Party.AB683-ASA4,6,24207. An entity that is controlled by the government of the People’s Republic of 21China, the Chinese Communist Party, the Chinese military, or an instrumentality 22of any of the foregoing, including the State-owned Assets Supervision and 23Administration Commission of the State Council of the People’s Republic of China 24and the National Council for Social Security Fund of the People’s Republic of China.AB683-ASA4,7,118. An agent of a person described in subds. 1. to 7.
