Referred to Committee on Senate Organization.SJR129,1,11Relating to: recognizing the holidays of Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha in Wisconsin.SJR129,1,32Whereas, Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha, collectively known as Eid, are the two 3major Islamic holidays; andSJR129,1,44Whereas, Eid translates to “holiday” or “festival” in Arabic; andSJR129,1,55Whereas, Islam is one of the world’s major religions; andSJR129,1,76Whereas, the first day of the 10th month of the Islamic calendar is globally 7recognized as Eid al-Fitr; andSJR129,1,108Whereas, Eid al-Fitr celebrates the conclusion of the holy month of Ramadan, 9during which Muslims fast from dawn to dusk, practice spiritual reflection, exercise 10self-discipline, and gather in community; andSJR129,1,1211Whereas, the 10th day of the 12th month of the Islamic calendar is globally 12recognized as Eid al-Adha; andSJR129,2,213Whereas, Eid al-Adha marks the end of the Hajj pilgrimage to Makkah, when 14Muslims commemorate Abraham’s willingness to sacrifice his son to show devotion 1to God, as referenced in the Quran, the Old Testament, and the New Testament; 2andSJR129,2,53Whereas, these holidays shift with the Islamic lunar calendar by 4approximately 10 to 11 days each year in accordance with visual sightings of the 5new crescent moon; andSJR129,2,86Whereas, the majority of Wisconsin’s 70,000 Muslims celebrate these two 7major holidays every year by attending community prayer, enjoying rich and festive 8meals, and spending time with friends and family; andSJR129,2,109Whereas, all Wisconsinites can take meaningful actions to recognize Eid in 10support of the diverse Muslim community in our state; andSJR129,2,1411Whereas, state recognition of Eid will highlight the holiday to schools and 12employers to underscore Eid’s religious and cultural importance to Muslims across 13the state, potentially enabling Muslim students and employees to fully observe 14their two major holidays; andSJR129,2,1615Whereas, Eid provides an occasion to foster a more welcoming and inclusive 16state by uplifting the Wisconsin Muslim community; now, therefore, be itSJR129,2,1917Resolved by the senate, the assembly concurring, That the Wisconsin 18Legislature hereby recognizes the holidays of Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha in 19Wisconsin.SJR129,2,2020(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/sjr129 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/sjr129 proposaltext/2025/REG/SJR129 proposaltext/2025/REG/SJR129 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»SJR129: Joint Resolution Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
