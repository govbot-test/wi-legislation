development. As a continuing 10appropriation, the amounts in the schedule to develop the food stamp platform 11under s. 49.79 (6w).AB180-ASA2,312Section 3. 20.435 (4) (bi) of the statutes is created to read:AB180-ASA2,2,141320.435 (4) (bi) Food stamp platform; administration. The amounts in the 14schedule to administer the food stamp platform under s. 49.79 (6w).AB180-ASA2,415Section 4. 49.78 (1m) (a) of the statutes is amended to read:AB180-ASA2,2,201649.78 (1m) (a) Except as provided in par. (c) No later than 6 months after the 17effective date of this paragraph .... [LRB inserts date], each county with a 18population of less than 750,000 shall participate in a multicounty consortium or a 19partnership with a Tribal income maintenance program that is approved by the 20department under par. (b).AB180-ASA2,521Section 5. 49.78 (1m) (c) of the statutes is amended to read:AB180-ASA2,3,62249.78 (1m) (c) If a county with a population of less than 750,000 does not 23participate in a multicounty consortium or the department determines that a 24multicounty consortium does not satisfy the department’s performance 1requirements, the department shall assume responsibility for administering 2income maintenance programs in that county or in the geographical area of the 3multicounty consortium. The department may provide income maintenance 4program administration under this paragraph by contracting with another 5multicounty consortium or by providing the administrative services with state 6resources and employees.AB180-ASA2,67Section 6. 49.78 (1m) (d) of the statutes is amended to read:AB180-ASA2,3,15849.78 (1m) (d) If the department assumes responsibility for administering 9income maintenance programs in a county or in the geographical area of the 10multicounty consortium under par. (c), any county for which the department 11administers income maintenance programs shall pay to the department the 12amount that the county expended for the administration of income maintenance 13programs in the calendar year 2009. For the purposes of this paragraph, Kenosha 14County expended $673,000 for the administration of income maintenance programs 15in calendar year 2009 preceding the department’s assumption of responsibility.AB180-ASA2,716Section 7. 49.79 (6w) of the statutes is created to read:AB180-ASA2,3,171749.79 (6w) Food stamp platform. (a) Definitions. In this subsection:AB180-ASA2,3,19181. “Eligibility” means the status of being eligible for purchase through the use 19of benefits under the food stamp program.AB180-ASA2,3,22202. “Nonprofit organization” means an organization that is described in section 21501 (c) (3) of the Internal Revenue Code and that is exempt from federal income tax 22under section 501 (a) of the Internal Revenue Code.AB180-ASA2,3,24233. “Participating retailer” means a retailer that participates in the food stamp 24program.AB180-ASA2,4,31(b) Contract for a food stamp platform. Subject to par. (d), the department 2shall enter into a contract for the development and administration of an electronic 3platform that has the capacity to do all of the following:AB180-ASA2,4,741. Determine whether a universal product code–marked product is eligible for 5purchase through the use of benefits under the food stamp program, subject to the 6list of eligible foods under 7 CFR 271.2 and any approved waiver of federal law from 7the U.S. department of agriculture.AB180-ASA2,4,1282. Contain a centralized database of universal product code–marked products 9that identifies which products are eligible for purchase through the use of benefits 10under the food stamp program, based upon the determinations under subd. 1. The 11centralized database under this subdivision shall have version control and audit 12functionality.AB180-ASA2,4,14133. Continuously update the database under subd. 2. with products that 14become newly commercially available.AB180-ASA2,4,16154. Continuously update the database under subd. 2. with products that are 16reformulated.AB180-ASA2,4,21175. Integrate the database under subd. 2. into point-of-sale systems used by 18participating retailers. The integration under this subdivision shall allow for 19automated data synchronization, real-time data checks, batch updates for low-20bandwidth or legacy systems, and minimal technical burden for small participating 21retailers.AB180-ASA2,4,23226. Include a documented application programming interface, batch file 23formats, a sandbox environment, and a help desk for small participating retailers.AB180-ASA2,5,2247. Comply with reporting requirements related to the food stamp program 1under federal law or an approved waiver of federal law from the U.S. department of 2agriculture.AB180-ASA2,5,438. Track and assess errors in product eligibility determinations by using audit 4tools and error-trend analysis.AB180-ASA2,5,65(c) Optional feature. The electronic platform under par. (b) may include a 6public-facing search function for the database under par. (b) 2.AB180-ASA2,5,97(d) Contract requirements. 1. The contract under par. (b) shall be a 8performance-based contract and shall include an approved budget for the 9development and administration of the electronic platform under par. (b).AB180-ASA2,5,11102. The department may enter into the contract under par. (b) only with a 11nonprofit organization that meets all of the following requirements: AB180-ASA2,5,1412a. Has a charitable and educational mission that includes support for the 13retail food industry in this state and research and industry education related to food 14safety and nutrition in a retail business.AB180-ASA2,5,1715b. Demonstrates statewide reach and working relationships with a wide 16variety of participating retailers, including participating retailers of varying sizes, 17and point-of-sale system vendors serving retailers in this state. AB180-ASA2,5,2218c. Maintains organizational independence and financial controls sufficient to 19ensure that grant funds awarded under this subsection are not used for lobbying, 20political activity, or private benefit, and if affiliated with an organization described 21in section 501 (c) (6) of the Internal Revenue Code, maintains separate boards or 22committees and separate accounting in accordance with federal law.AB180-ASA2,6,223d. Commits to providing retailer-facing training, technical assistance, and 1outreach, including by providing materials accessible to small retailers, to support 2accurate product eligibility classification at the point of sale.AB180-ASA2,6,73(e) Partnership with technology firm. The nonprofit organization that 4contracts with the department under par. (b) shall partner with a technology firm 5that is experienced with this state’s Medical Assistance enterprise data warehouse 6and data analytics reporting system for the administration of the electronic 7platform under par. (b).AB180-ASA2,6,148(f) Reporting required. By no later than September 30 of the fiscal year 9beginning after the department enters into the contract with the nonprofit 10organization under par. (b), and annually by no later than September 30 thereafter, 11the nonprofit organization shall submit a report to the department and the 12appropriate standing committees of the legislature under s. 13.172 (3) on all of the 13following information about the electronic platform under par. (b) from the 14immediately preceding fiscal year:AB180-ASA2,6,16151. The number of unique universal product codes in the database under par. 16(b) 2.AB180-ASA2,6,19172. The number of products added to and removed from the database under 18par. (b) 2. and the number of products for which the information in the database 19was updated due to reformulation.AB180-ASA2,6,21203. The accuracy rate, including false positive and false negative rates, of the 21platform’s product eligibility determinations.AB180-ASA2,6,23224. The cycle time to publish additions and updates under par. (b) 3. and 4. to 23participating retailers.AB180-ASA2,7,215. The number and percentage of participating retailers using the platform, 2disaggregated by all of the following:AB180-ASA2,7,33a. Participating retailers’ store count.AB180-ASA2,7,54b. Whether a participating retailer is independently operated or a part of a 5chain of retailers.AB180-ASA2,7,66c. Whether a participating retailer is located in an urban or rural setting.AB180-ASA2,7,976. Uptime, scheduled and unscheduled outages, incident counts and 8severities, mean time to resolution, and the results of any cybersecurity testing and 9third party risk assessments.AB180-ASA2,7,11107. The error-trend analysis required under par. (b) 8. for the platform’s errors 11in product eligibility determinations, including all of the following:AB180-ASA2,7,1312a. Any early warnings triggered, corrective actions taken, and the outcomes of 13those actions.AB180-ASA2,7,1514b. A summary of audit trail activity and version control statistics 15demonstrating a clear historical record of changes.AB180-ASA2,7,20168. A description of how the platform supported compliance with applicable 17federal requirements and any approved waiver of federal law from the U.S. 18department of agriculture, including metrics showing the platform’s contribution to 19the reduction of improper payments and benefit denials caused by errors in product 20eligibility determinations.AB180-ASA2,7,24219. The volume and resolution of participating retailer and point-of-sale 22system vendor support tickets, a description of training delivered to participating 23retailers, and the results of any user satisfaction survey conducted by the nonprofit 24organization.AB180-ASA2,8,3110. Confirmation of adherence to data governance requirements under state 2law and any applicable confidentiality requirements under federal law, a summary 3of any data sharing agreements in effect, and any privacy incidents and responses.
