ASSEMBLY SUBSTITUTE AMENDMENT 2,
TO ASSEMBLY BILL 180
February 19, 2026 - Offered by Representative Moses.
AB180-ASA2,1,7
1An Act to amend 49.78 (1m) (a), 49.78 (1m) (c) and 49.78 (1m) (d); to create
220.435 (4) (bh), 20.435 (4) (bi), 49.79 (6w) and 49.79 (7x) of the statutes;
3relating to: requiring the Department of Health Services to seek any
4necessary waiver to prohibit the purchase of candy or soft drinks with food
5stamp program benefits, establishing an electronic platform for identifying
6food stamp program–eligible products, food stamp program administration,
7and making an appropriation.
The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:

11under s. 49.79 (6w).

AB180-ASA2,716Section 7. 49.79 (6w) of the statutes is created to read:
AB180-ASA2,3,171749.79 (6w) Food stamp platform. (a) Definitions. In this subsection:
AB180-ASA2,3,19181. “Eligibility” means the status of being eligible for purchase through the use
19of benefits under the food stamp program.
AB180-ASA2,3,22202. “Nonprofit organization” means an organization that is described in section
21501 (c) (3) of the Internal Revenue Code and that is exempt from federal income tax
22under section 501 (a) of the Internal Revenue Code.
AB180-ASA2,3,24233. “Participating retailer” means a retailer that participates in the food stamp
24program.
AB180-ASA2,4,3
1(b) Contract for a food stamp platform. Subject to par. (d), the department
2shall enter into a contract for the development and administration of an electronic
3platform that has the capacity to do all of the following:
AB180-ASA2,4,741. Determine whether a universal product code–marked product is eligible for
5purchase through the use of benefits under the food stamp program, subject to the
6list of eligible foods under 7 CFR 271.2 and any approved waiver of federal law from
7the U.S. department of agriculture.
AB180-ASA2,4,1282. Contain a centralized database of universal product code–marked products
9that identifies which products are eligible for purchase through the use of benefits
10under the food stamp program, based upon the determinations under subd. 1. The
11centralized database under this subdivision shall have version control and audit
12functionality.
AB180-ASA2,4,14133. Continuously update the database under subd. 2. with products that
14become newly commercially available.
AB180-ASA2,4,16154. Continuously update the database under subd. 2. with products that are
16reformulated.
AB180-ASA2,4,21175. Integrate the database under subd. 2. into point-of-sale systems used by
18participating retailers. The integration under this subdivision shall allow for
19automated data synchronization, real-time data checks, batch updates for low-
20bandwidth or legacy systems, and minimal technical burden for small participating
21retailers.
AB180-ASA2,4,23226. Include a documented application programming interface, batch file
23formats, a sandbox environment, and a help desk for small participating retailers.
AB180-ASA2,5,2247. Comply with reporting requirements related to the food stamp program
1under federal law or an approved waiver of federal law from the U.S. department of
2agriculture.
AB180-ASA2,5,438. Track and assess errors in product eligibility determinations by using audit
4tools and error-trend analysis.
AB180-ASA2,5,65(c) Optional feature. The electronic platform under par. (b) may include a
6public-facing search function for the database under par. (b) 2.
AB180-ASA2,5,97(d) Contract requirements. 1. The contract under par. (b) shall be a
8performance-based contract and shall include an approved budget for the
9development and administration of the electronic platform under par. (b).
AB180-ASA2,5,11102. The department may enter into the contract under par. (b) only with a
11nonprofit organization that meets all of the following requirements:
AB180-ASA2,5,1412a. Has a charitable and educational mission that includes support for the
13retail food industry in this state and research and industry education related to food
14safety and nutrition in a retail business.
AB180-ASA2,5,1715b. Demonstrates statewide reach and working relationships with a wide
16variety of participating retailers, including participating retailers of varying sizes,
17and point-of-sale system vendors serving retailers in this state.
AB180-ASA2,5,2218c. Maintains organizational independence and financial controls sufficient to
19ensure that grant funds awarded under this subsection are not used for lobbying,
20political activity, or private benefit, and if affiliated with an organization described
21in section 501 (c) (6) of the Internal Revenue Code, maintains separate boards or
22committees and separate accounting in accordance with federal law.
AB180-ASA2,6,223d. Commits to providing retailer-facing training, technical assistance, and
1outreach, including by providing materials accessible to small retailers, to support
2accurate product eligibility classification at the point of sale.
AB180-ASA2,6,73(e) Partnership with technology firm. The nonprofit organization that
4contracts with the department under par. (b) shall partner with a technology firm
5that is experienced with this state’s Medical Assistance enterprise data warehouse
6and data analytics reporting system for the administration of the electronic
7platform under par. (b).

10organization under par. (b), and annually by no later than September 30 thereafter,

12appropriate standing committees of the legislature under s. 13.172 (3) on all of the
13following information about the electronic platform under par. (b) from the
14immediately preceding fiscal year:
AB180-ASA2,6,16151. The number of unique universal product codes in the database under par.
16(b) 2.
AB180-ASA2,6,19172. The number of products added to and removed from the database under
18par. (b) 2. and the number of products for which the information in the database
19was updated due to reformulation.
AB180-ASA2,6,21203. The accuracy rate, including false positive and false negative rates, of the
21platform’s product eligibility determinations.
AB180-ASA2,6,23224. The cycle time to publish additions and updates under par. (b) 3. and 4. to
23participating retailers.
