relating to: fees related to animal markets, animal 1dealers, animal truckers, animal transport vehicles, and animal health and 2license registrations.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauCurrent law requires the Department of Agriculture, Trade and Consumer Protection to specify by rule fees related to animal market licenses, animal dealer licenses, animal trucker licenses, animal transport vehicle registrations, and the license registration of bovine animals, swine, equine animals, poultry and farm-raised game birds, farm-raised deer, fish farms, sheep, goats, and feed lots. This bill removes DATCP’s authority to promulgate rules regarding the fees and statutorily establishes the fees in the same amounts as DATCP had established for the fees by rule, as published in the Wisconsin Administrative Register in December 2024.The bill also requires DATCP to submit an annual report to the Joint Committee on Finance and the appropriate standing committees of the legislature that details the revenue generated from the fees under the bill and the expenditures of that revenue in the preceding fiscal year.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB622-ASA7,13Section 1. 93.01 (7m) of the statutes is created to read:SB622-ASA7,2,8493.01 (7m) “Intermediate livestock handling facility” means an off-premises 5facility owned or leased by a slaughtering establishment that is licensed by the 6department or that is subject to inspection by the U.S. department of agriculture 7and used to unload and temporarily hold animals before the animals are delivered 8to the slaughtering establishment.SB622-ASA7,29Section 2. 93.06 (1g) of the statutes is amended to read:SB622-ASA7,3,21093.06 (1g) Certificates of veterinary inspection. Furnish, to 11veterinarians in this state, forms to be used by them in issuing certificates of 12veterinary inspection. The department may charge a $2 fee for each form unless 13the department specifies a different fee by rule fee of $5.60 for each interstate 1movement form, $1 for each interstate movement form continuation, and 60 cents 2for each intrastate movement form.SB622-ASA7,33Section 3. 93.06 (1n) (c) of the statutes is repealed.SB622-ASA7,44Section 4. 93.07 (10g) of the statutes is created to read:SB622-ASA7,3,8593.07 (10g) Animal health fees. No later than August 1 of each year, to 6submit to the joint committee on finance and to the appropriate standing 7committees of the legislature in the manner provided under s. 13.172 (3) a report 8detailing all of the following:SB622-ASA7,3,139(a) The revenue generated from fees related to animal market licenses, animal 10dealer licenses, animal trucker licenses, animal transport vehicle registrations, and 11the license registration of bovine animals, swine, equine animals, poultry and farm-12raised game birds, farm-raised deer, fish farms, sheep, goats, and feed lots in the 13preceding fiscal year.SB622-ASA7,3,1514(b) The expenditures of any revenue generated under par. (a) in the preceding 15fiscal year.SB622-ASA7,516Section 5. 95.25 (7) of the statutes is created to read:SB622-ASA7,3,221795.25 (7) (a) Upon application, the department may certify a herd of bovine 18animals as an accredited tuberculosis-free herd if the herd qualifies for that 19certification under the tuberculosis uniform methods determined by the 20department. Every certification application under this paragraph shall include a 21nonrefundable application fee of $100 for a 2-year certification. The department 22may prorate the fee for certifications of less than 2 years.SB622-ASA7,4,623(b) Upon application, the department may certify a herd of farm-raised deer 24as an accredited tuberculosis-free herd, a tuberculosis-monitored herd, or a 1tuberculosis-qualified herd, if the herd qualifies for that certification under 9 CFR 277. To acquire and maintain certification, a keeper of farm-raised deer shall comply 3with applicable requirements under the tuberculosis uniform methods determined 4by the department. Every certification application under this paragraph shall 5include a nonrefundable application fee of $150 for a 3-year certification. The 6department may prorate the fee for certifications of less than 3 years.SB622-ASA7,4,117(c) Upon application, the department may certify a herd of goats as an 8accredited tuberculosis-free herd if the herd qualifies for that certification using the 9bovine tuberculosis uniform methods determined by the department. Every 10certification application under this paragraph shall include a nonrefundable 11application fee of $50.SB622-ASA7,4,1412(d) To maintain a certification under this subsection, a herd owner shall 13comply with applicable requirements under the tuberculosis uniform methods as 14determined by the department.SB622-ASA7,615Section 6. 95.26 (8) of the statutes is created to read:SB622-ASA7,4,201695.26 (8) (a) Upon application, the department may annually certify a herd of 17bovine animals, herd of swine, herd of goat, or sheep flock as a brucellosis-free herd 18or flock if the herd or flock qualifies for that certification under the brucellosis 19uniform methods determined by department. Every certification application under 20this paragraph shall include a nonrefundable fee of $50 for an annual certification. SB622-ASA7,5,221(b) Upon application, the department may certify a herd of farm-raised deer 22as a brucellosis-free herd if the herd qualifies for that certification under the 23brucellosis uniform methods determined by department. Every certification 24application under this paragraph shall include a nonrefundable fee of $150 for a 3-1year certification. The department may prorate the fee for certifications of less 2than 3 years.SB622-ASA7,5,53(c) To maintain a certification under this subsection, a herd or flock owner 4shall comply with applicable requirements under the brucellosis uniform methods 5as determined by the department.SB622-ASA7,76Section 7. 95.27 (7m) of the statutes is created to read:SB622-ASA7,5,13795.27 (7m) Upon application, the department may annually certify a herd of 8swine as a qualified pseudorabies-negative herd if the herd qualifies for that 9certification under the pseudorabies national eradication standards. Every 10certification application under this paragraph shall include a nonrefundable fee of 11$50 for an annual certification. To maintain the certification, the herd owner shall 12comply with applicable requirements under the pseudorabies national eradication 13standards.SB622-ASA7,814Section 8. 95.27 (8) of the statutes is repealed.SB622-ASA7,915Section 9. 95.51 (2) (c) of the statutes is created to read:SB622-ASA7,5,201695.51 (2) (c) 1. The department may issue a certificate designating a facility 17as an approved intermediate livestock handling facility for purposes of this section. 18To obtain certification as an intermediate livestock handling facility, a facility 19operator shall submit an application on a form provided by the department. The 20application shall include a nonrefundable fee of $140.SB622-ASA7,5,22212. A person who applies for a certificate after that certificate has expired shall 22pay, in addition to the fee required subd. 1., a $28 late fee.SB622-ASA7,1023Section 10. 95.51 (9) of the statutes is created to read:SB622-ASA7,6,32495.51 (9) Equine infectious anemia. (a) No person may move an equine 1animal that tests positive for equine infectious anemia, except as provided by the 2department. The department shall summarily quarantine every test-positive 3animal and serve the owner with a quarantine notice.SB622-ASA7,6,74(b) The department shall retest an animal quarantined under par. (a) if, 5within 10 days after the quarantine notice is served, the owner of the quarantined 6animal files a written retest request with the department and pays a retest fee of 7$25.SB622-ASA7,118Section 11. 95.51 (10) of the statutes is created to read:SB622-ASA7,6,12995.51 (10) Foreign equine imports; quarantine station. (a) No person 10may receive in this state any stallion or mare imported from another nation in 11which contagious equine metritis has been reported unless all of the following 12apply:SB622-ASA7,6,17131. The stallion or mare is imported directly to an equine quarantine station 14approved under par. (b) in a sealed vehicle that has been sealed at a place, and by an 15agent, approved by the federal government. The vehicle seal may not be removed, 16except at the approved equine quarantine station by an authorized employee or 17agent of the department.SB622-ASA7,6,21182. The person applies for a permit to receive the stallion or mare and pays a 19nonrefundable $100 application fee and the department issues the permit 20authorizing the import shipment. A copy of the permit shall accompany the 21shipment.SB622-ASA7,7,322(b) An imported stallion or mare received at an approved equine quarantine 23station shall be automatically quarantined until the department releases the 24quarantine. A quarantine station does not qualify as an approved equine 1quarantine station unless the operator holds a current annual permit issued by the 2department. An application for a permit under this paragraph shall include a 3nonrefundable annual permit fee of $100.SB622-ASA7,124Section 12. 95.55 (3) (a) of the statutes is renumbered 95.55 (3) (a) (intro.) 5and amended to read:SB622-ASA7,7,10695.55 (3) (a) (intro.) The department shall, by rule, specify the fee for impose 7the following nonrefundable registration fees for a herd of farm-raised deer under 8this section. The, except that the department may not require an individual who is 9eligible for the veterans fee waiver program under s. 45.44 to pay a fee for 10registration under this section.:SB622-ASA7,1311Section 13. 95.55 (3) (a) 1. to 3. of the statutes are created to read:SB622-ASA7,7,131295.55 (3) (a) 1. An annual fee of $85 if the herd includes no more than 15 farm-13raised deer and the following criteria are met:SB622-ASA7,7,1514a. No live farm-raised deer move from the premises except directly to 15slaughter.SB622-ASA7,7,1916b. The farm-raised deer are killed on the premises for consumption by the 17farm-raised deer keeper or are slaughtered on the premises provided that the 18department conducts an ante-mortem inspection of the deer and a post-mortem 19inspection of the carcass under s. 97.42.SB622-ASA7,7,2120c. The herd is not enrolled in the chronic wasting disease herd status program 21maintained by the department.SB622-ASA7,7,23222. An annual fee of $162 if the herd includes no more than 15 farm-raised 23deer, and the herd does not meet all of the criteria under subd. 1.SB622-ASA7,7,24243. An annual fee of $325 if the herd includes more than 15 farm-raised deer.SB622-ASA7,141Section 14. 95.55 (3) (am) of the statutes is created to read:SB622-ASA7,8,5295.55 (3) (am) 1. A person who applies to register a medically separated farm-3raised deer herd at the same location where another farm-raised deer herd is 4registered shall pay a nonrefundable fee of $400 a day, or portion of a day, needed to 5complete the inspection required by the department.SB622-ASA7,8,1262. An applicant for an annual registration certificate shall pay a registration 7fee surcharge of $250 if the department determines that, within 365 days prior to 8submitting a complete registration application, the applicant kept farm-raised deer 9at any location without a required registration certificate that identifies that 10location. In addition to the surcharge, the applicant shall pay the fee due under par. 11(a) for the year in which the applicant failed to obtain the required registration 12certificate.SB622-ASA7,8,15133. A person who applies for an annual registration certificate after that 14certificate has expired shall pay, in addition to all other fees required under this 15subsection, a late fee equal to 20 percent of the registration fees.
