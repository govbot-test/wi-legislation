Referred to Committee on Agriculture and Revenue.SB622,1,81An Act to repeal 95.68 (4) (b) 2., 95.69 (4) (b) 2. and 95.71 (5) (c) 2.; to 2renumber 95.68 (7) and 95.69 (7); to renumber and amend 95.68 (4) (a); to 3amend 95.68 (4) (b) 1., 95.68 (8), 95.69 (4) (a), 95.69 (4) (b) 1., 95.69 (8), 95.71 4(5) (a), 95.71 (5) (b), 95.71 (5) (c) 1. and 95.71 (8); to create 95.68 (1) (aj), 95.68 5(1) (ak), 95.68 (1) (aL), 95.68 (4) (a) 1., 95.68 (4) (a) 2., 95.68 (4) (a) 3., 95.68 (7) 6(b), 95.69 (5) (a) 3., 95.69 (5) (a) 4., 95.69 (5) (a) 5. and 95.69 (7) (b) of the 7statutes; relating to: fees related to animal markets, animal dealers, animal 8truckers, and animal transport vehicles.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauUnder current law, the Department of Agriculture, Trade and Consumer Protection is required to specify by rule fees related to animal market licenses, animal dealer licenses, animal trucker licenses, and animal transport vehicle registrations. This bill removes DATCP’s authority to promulgate rules regarding the fees and statutorily establishes the fees in the same amounts as DATCP had established for the fees by rule, as published in the Wisconsin Administrative Register in December 2024.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB622,11Section 1. 95.68 (1) (aj) of the statutes is created to read:SB622,2,5295.68 (1) (aj) “Class A animal market license” means an annual license 3authorizing a person to operate an animal market at which the person may conduct 4livestock and wild animal sales and auction livestock on any number of days during 5the license year.SB622,26Section 2. 95.68 (1) (ak) of the statutes is created to read:SB622,2,11795.68 (1) (ak) “Class B animal market license” means an annual license 8authorizing a person to operate an animal market at which the person may conduct 9livestock sales on any number of days during the license year and auction livestock 10on no more than 4 days during the license year. A Class B animal market license 11does not authorize a person to conduct a sale or auction of a wild animal.SB622,312Section 3. 95.68 (1) (aL) of the statutes is created to read:SB622,2,181395.68 (1) (aL) “Class E animal market license” means an annual license 14authorizing a person to operate an animal market at which the person may conduct 15sales of equine animals on any number of days during the license year. A Class E 16animal market license does not authorize a person to conduct an auction of an 17equine animal. A Class E animal market license does not authorize a person to 18conduct a sale or auction of a wild animal or livestock that is not an equine animal.
