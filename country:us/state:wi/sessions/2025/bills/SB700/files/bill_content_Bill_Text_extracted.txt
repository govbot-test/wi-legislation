Referred to Committee on Transportation and Local Government.SB700,1,31An Act to amend 350.12 (3) (a) 1., 350.12 (3j) (b), 350.12 (3j) (bg) 3. and 350.12 2(3j) (bg) 4. of the statutes; relating to: fees for snowmobile registration and 3trail use stickers.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill increases the fees for snowmobile registration and trail use stickers. The bill increases the fee for a snowmobile public use registration certificate from $30 to $45. The bill increases fees for a snowmobile trail use sticker as follows:1. For a snowmobile registered in-state, from $29.25 to $49.25.2. For a snowmobile registered in-state but owned by a person who is both a member of a snowmobile club and a member of the Association of Wisconsin Snowmobile Clubs, from $9.25 to $19.25.3. For a snowmobile registered out-of-state, from $49.25 to $69.25.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB700,14Section 1. 350.12 (3) (a) 1. of the statutes is amended to read:SB700,2,191350.12 (3) (a) 1. (b) and has the 5registration decals displayed as required under sub. (5) or s. 350.122 or unless the 6snowmobile has a plate and a registration decal attached as required under par. (c) 73. A snowmobile that is not registered as an antique under par. (b) may be 8registered for public use. A snowmobile that is not registered as an antique under 9par. (b) and that is used exclusively on private property, as defined under s. 23.33 (1) 10(n), may be registered for private use. A snowmobile public-use registration 11certificate is valid for 3 years beginning on the July 1 prior to the date of application 12if registration is made prior to April 1 and beginning on the July 1 subsequent to 13the date of application if registration is made after April 1 and ending on June 30, 143 years thereafter. A snowmobile private-use registration certificate is valid from 15the date of issuance until ownership of the snowmobile is transferred. The fee for 16the issuance or renewal of a public-use registration certificate is $30 $45, except 17that the fee is $5 if it is a snowmobile owned and operated by a political subdivision 18of this state.
