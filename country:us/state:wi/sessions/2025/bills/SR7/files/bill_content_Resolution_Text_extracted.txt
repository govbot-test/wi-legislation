Referred to Committee on Senate Organization.SR7,1,21Relating to: convening additional senate floor periods to address affordability for 2Wisconsin.SR7,1,53Whereas, Senate Joint Resolution 1 was passed by the Wisconsin Senate on 4January 6, 2025, and concurred in by the Wisconsin Assembly on January 6, 2025, 5and enrolled as Senate Joint Resolution 2 on May 20, 2025; andSR7,1,86Whereas, the senate is anticipated to adjourn on March 17, 2026, and not 7reconvene the regular session until the start of the 2027–28 biennial session in 8January 2027; andSR7,1,119Whereas, including the March 17, 2026, floor session, the senate has convened 1012 regular session floor periods and one extraordinary session floor period since 11January 6, 2025; andSR7,1,1412Whereas, in recent years, senate committees have expanded the use of paper 13ballots for holding executive session votes, forgoing debate and opportunities for 14amendments; andSR7,2,21Whereas, the fewest number of acts will have passed into law upon 2adjournment of the 2025–26 legislative session in the past 25 years; andSR7,2,73Whereas, U.S. President Donald John Trump has increased tariffs by 4executive order on imports contributing to rising costs of goods for Wisconsinites, 5and in response, countries around the world have levied reciprocating tariffs on 6American exports, thereby shrinking markets for Wisconsin businesses and their 7ability to compete globally; andSR7,2,108Whereas, U.S. President Donald John Trump’s decision to execute Operation 9Epic Fury against Iran on February 28, 2026, has destabilized the region and is 10contributing to higher gasoline and diesel prices globally; andSR7,2,1411Whereas, U.S. President Donald John Trump signed the One Big Beautiful 12Bill into law on July 4, 2025, which ends the Affordable Care Act enhanced 13subsidies, thereby contributing to higher health care premiums for all Americans; 14andSR7,2,1815Whereas, the Wisconsin Child Care Bridge payments are set to expire on June 1628, 2026, leaving child care centers unable to afford the cost of inflation, inevitably 17closing their businesses and forcing parents to drop out of the workforce to care for 18their children; andSR7,2,2019Whereas, seniors with fixed incomes are unable to afford inflationary price 20increases of essential goods to make ends meet; andSR7,2,2321Whereas, utility rates are expected to increase by double digit percentages, 22forcing renters and homeowners to pay more for their electric and water utility 23services; andSR7,3,224Whereas, farmers are facing lower prices for their harvests from last year and 1higher costs for planting this spring, thereby contributing to higher costs of 2groceries for consumers; andSR7,3,53Whereas, inflation continues to rise and prices for all consumer goods are 4increasing, forcing businesses to lay off workers and prepare for another global 5economic downturn; andSR7,3,86Whereas, as of March 16, 2026, members of the minority party have 7introduced 378 senate bills with a minority party member as first author, many of 8which would lower costs for Wisconsinites; andSR7,3,119Whereas, as of March 16, 2026, the majority party members have scheduled 10only nine senate bills with a minority party member as first author for a committee 11public hearing; andSR7,3,1412Whereas, as of March 16, 2026, the majority party members have scheduled 13only three senate bills with a minority party member as first author for a 14committee executive session; andSR7,3,1715Whereas, as of March 16, 2026, the majority-controlled Senate Committee on 16Organization has scheduled no senate bills with a minority party member as first 17author for a vote on the senate floor; andSR7,3,1918Whereas, as of March 16, 2026, none of the senate bills with a minority party 19member as first author have been passed by the senate; andSR7,3,2120Whereas, the senate must convene additional senate floor periods to address 21affordability for Wisconsin; now, therefore, be itSR7,4,222Resolved by the senate, That the Wisconsin Senate shall schedule at least 23one regular senate floor period during the limited-business floor period of April 14, 12026, to April 16, 2026, to address affordability and pass senate bills to lower costs 2for Wisconsinites; and, be it furtherSR7,4,53Resolved, That the Wisconsin Senate shall schedule extraordinary sessions 4to address affordability and pass senate bills to lower costs for Wisconsinites as 5needed until December 31, 2026.SR7,4,66(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/sr7 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/sr7 proposaltext/2025/REG/SR7 proposaltext/2025/REG/SR7 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»SR7: Resolution Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
