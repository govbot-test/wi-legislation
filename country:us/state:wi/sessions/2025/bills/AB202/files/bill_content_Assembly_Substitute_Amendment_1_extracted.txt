to create 421.103 (5), 704.445 and 704.446 of the statutes; 4relating to: voidable provisions in residential rental agreements, review of 5standardized residential rental agreements, and the application of the 6Wisconsin Consumer Act to residential rental agreements.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB202-ASA1,17Section 1. 421.103 (5) of the statutes is created to read:AB202-ASA1,1,98421.103 (5) Chapters 421 to 427 do not apply to residential rental agreements 9governed under s. 710.15 or ch. 704.AB202-ASA1,210Section 2. 704.44 (intro.) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,2,61704.44 Residential rental agreement that contains certain provisions 2is void. (intro.) Notwithstanding s. 704.02, a residential rental agreement is void 3and unenforceable if it and subject to s. 704.445, a tenant may elect to void a 4residential rental agreement, or sever a prohibited provision from a residential 5rental agreement, if the residential rental agreement does any of the following, all 6of which are prohibited provisions for purposes of this section and s. 704.445:AB202-ASA1,37Section 3. 704.44 (1m) (intro.) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,2,108704.44 (1m) (intro.) Allows By its express language specifically authorizes a 9landlord to do any of the following because a tenant has contacted an entity for law 10enforcement services, health services, or safety services:AB202-ASA1,411Section 4. 704.44 (2m) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,2,1412704.44 (2m) Authorizes By its express language specifically authorizes the 13eviction or exclusion of a tenant from the premises, other than by judicial eviction 14procedures as provided under ch. 799.AB202-ASA1,515Section 5. 704.44 (3m) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,2,1916704.44 (3m) Provides By its express language specifically provides for an 17acceleration of rent payments in the event of tenant default or breach of obligations 18under the rental agreement, or otherwise waives the landlord’s obligation to 19mitigate damages as provided in s. 704.29.AB202-ASA1,620Section 6. 704.44 (4m) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,3,221704.44 (4m) Requires By its express language specifically requires payment 22by the tenant of attorney fees or costs incurred by the landlord in any legal action or 23dispute arising under the rental agreement. This subsection does not prevent a 1landlord or tenant from recovering costs or attorney fees under a court order under 2ch. 799 or 814.AB202-ASA1,73Section 7. 704.44 (5m) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,3,64704.44 (5m) Authorizes By its express language specifically authorizes the 5landlord or an agent of the landlord to confess judgment against the tenant in any 6action arising under the rental agreement.AB202-ASA1,87Section 8. 704.44 (6) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,3,128704.44 (6) States By its express language specifically states that the landlord 9is not liable for property damage or personal injury caused by negligent acts or 10omissions of the landlord. This subsection does not affect ordinary maintenance 11obligations of a tenant under s. 704.07 or assumed by a tenant under a rental 12agreement or other written agreement between the landlord and the tenant.AB202-ASA1,913Section 9. 704.44 (7) (intro.) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,3,1514704.44 (7) (intro.) Imposes By its express language specifically imposes 15liability on a tenant for any of the following:AB202-ASA1,1016Section 10. 704.44 (8) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,3,1917704.44 (8) Waives By its express language specifically waives any statutory or 18other legal obligation on the part of the landlord to deliver the premises in a fit or 19habitable condition or to maintain the premises during the tenant’s tenancy.AB202-ASA1,1120Section 11. 704.44 (9) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,3,2421704.44 (9) Allows By its express language specifically authorizes the landlord 22to terminate the tenancy of a tenant based solely on the commission of a crime in or 23on the rental property if the tenant, or someone who lawfully resides with the 24tenant, is the victim, as defined in s. 950.02 (4), of that crime.AB202-ASA1,121Section 12. 704.44 (10) of the statutes is amended to read:AB202-ASA1,4,52704.44 (10) Allows By its express language specifically authorizes the 3landlord to terminate the tenancy of a tenant for a crime committed in relation to 4the rental property and the rental agreement does not include the notice required 5under s. 704.14.AB202-ASA1,136Section 13. 704.445 of the statutes is created to read:AB202-ASA1,4,137704.445 Remedies for voidable residential rental agreements. (1) If a 8tenant elects to void the tenant’s residential agreement under s. 704.44, the 9tenant’s tenancy shall become a periodic tenancy as defined under s. 704.01 (2), and 10the tenant shall be responsible for the payment of rent, utilities, parking, and other 11charges under terms and conditions identical to the terms and conditions as set 12forth in the original residential rental agreement, but not including a provision 13described under s. 704.44 (1m) to (10).AB202-ASA1,4,1614(2) If a tenant elects to sever a prohibited provision from his or her residential 15rental agreement as provided under s. 704.44, the unsevered portion of the 16residential rental agreement shall be given full effect.AB202-ASA1,4,2017(3) A tenant’s election to void or sever a prohibited provision from his or her 18residential rental agreement as provided under s. 704.44 is effective upon the 19tenant giving notice of the election to the landlord in the same manner as specified 20in s. 704.21 (2).AB202-ASA1,5,821(4) A person suffering pecuniary loss directly caused by a residential rental 22agreement’s inclusion of a provision described under s. 704.44 (1m) to (10) may sue 23for damages therefor in any court of competent jurisdiction and shall recover twice 24the amount of such pecuniary loss, together with costs, including a reasonable 1attorney fee. There is a presumption that payments for rent, parking, and utilities 2are made by tenants in exchange for the value of the possession of the premises and 3usage of the utilities and are not pecuniary losses. This presumption may be 4rebutted by proof in the greater weight of the credible evidence that a tenant has 5incurred a pecuniary loss directly caused by a residential rental agreement's 6inclusion of a provision described under s. 704.44 (1m) to (10), and that such 7inclusion resulted in the amount the tenant paid to the landlord being greater than 8the value of the possession of the premises and usage of the utilities by the tenant.AB202-ASA1,5,119(5) A person suffering a pecuniary loss caused by a residential rental 10agreement’s inclusion of a provision described under s. 704.44 (1m) to (10) is 11limited to the remedies provided under this section.AB202-ASA1,1412Section 14. 704.446 of the statutes is created to read:AB202-ASA1,5,1413704.446 Review of standardized residential rental agreements. (1) 14Definitions. In this section:AB202-ASA1,5,1615(a) “Department” means the department of agriculture, trade and consumer 16protection.AB202-ASA1,5,1817(b) “Rental agreement” means a residential rental agreement together with 18any related addendum.AB202-ASA1,5,2119(2) Department review. (a) 1. Subject to par. (c), the department shall 20review all of the following standardized rental agreements for compliance with the 21law of this state:AB202-ASA1,5,2222a. Wisconsin Realtors Association residential rental agreement forms.AB202-ASA1,5,2323b. Wisconsin Legal Blank's residential rental agreement forms.AB202-ASA1,5,2424c. National Apartment Association residential rental agreement forms.AB202-ASA1,6,11d. Wisconsin Housing Alliance residential rental agreement forms.AB202-ASA1,6,32e. Federal department of housing and urban development or assisted housing 3residential rental agreement forms.AB202-ASA1,6,642. If a rental agreement previously reviewed by the department is 5substantially revised, the department shall review the substantially revised rental 6agreement within 60 days of its submission to the department.AB202-ASA1,6,873. The department may review a revised rental agreement only once annually 8unless a revision is triggered by a change in law or a published court decision.AB202-ASA1,6,1694. Upon review of a rental agreement, the department shall identify to the 10drafter of the rental agreement provisions the department deems noncompliant 11with the law of this state. If the drafter makes changes to these identified 12provisions, the department shall review the changes and provide a decision if the 13rental agreement is compliant with the law of this state within 14 days of 14submission of the changes. If the department does not identify any noncompliant 15provisions in a submitted rental agreement, the rental agreement is considered 16compliant with the law of this state.AB202-ASA1,6,2417(b) Subject to par. (c), the department may review for compliance with the law 18of this state rental agreements other than those under par. (a) 1. that are submitted 19to the department by attorneys licensed to practice law in this state or 20organizations that provide leases to landlords and tenants in this state. These 21rental agreements shall be reviewed as provided in par. (a) 4. If a reviewed rental 22agreement under this paragraph is compliant with the law of this state, the 23department may include the rental agreement in the list of rental agreements 24determined compliant and published under sub. (4).AB202-ASA1,7,21(c) A rental agreement must include a clear date of creation and a form 2number or title identifying the version of the agreement.AB202-ASA1,7,63(d) After the department reviews a rental agreement under par. (a) or (b), the 4version deemed compliant remains in effect and sub. (3) applies until a change in 5the law of this state relating to residential landlord-tenant relationships materially 6affects the content or enforceability of the rental agreement.AB202-ASA1,7,97(3) Safe harbor. (a) The use of a rental agreement deemed compliant under 8sub. (2) constitutes compliance with this chapter and rules promulgated by the 9department governing residential landlord-tenant relationships.AB202-ASA1,7,1310(b) A landlord or tenant who uses a rental agreement reviewed under sub. (2) 11is not subject to enforcement under s. 100.20 or 100.26, or any rule promulgated by 12the department under s. 100.20 or 100.26, for an alleged violation that is based on 13language contained in the reviewed rental agreement.AB202-ASA1,7,1714(c) The completion of designated blank spaces that are intended to be 15completed in a rental agreement reviewed under sub. (2), in a manner contemplated 16by the form, does not constitute a material modification and does not affect the 17application of the safe harbor under this subsection.AB202-ASA1,7,2218(d) Any other modification to a rental agreement reviewed under sub. (2), or 19the addition of language not expressly provided for in the reviewed rental 20agreement, including language inserted outside designated blank spaces, is made 21at the user's own risk and this subsection may not apply to the additional or 22modified language only.
