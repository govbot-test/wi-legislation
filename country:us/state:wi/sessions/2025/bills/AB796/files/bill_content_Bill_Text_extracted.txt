Referred to Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety.AB796,1,31An Act to create 20.465 (3) (cm) and (cp) and 323.64 of the statutes; relating 2to: grants to nonprofit religious organizations for security purposes and 3making an appropriation.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill requires the Department of Military Affairs to award grants to nonprofit religious organizations for security purposes. The bill sets a cap of $500,000 per recipient in each fiscal biennium. Under the bill, DMA must give priority to organizations that have been or are at risk of being a target of an incident or crime motivated by the actor’s bias against a religion or religious group. Under the bill, the grant money may be used to enhance the physical security of the organization’s location, to train or hire individuals to improve crisis response capabilities or provide security, and to prepare for an emergency. The bill also provides two positions at DMA to administer the grant program.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB796,11Section 1. 20.005 (3) (schedule) of the statutes: at the appropriate place, 2insert the following amounts for the purposes indicated: - See PDF for table AB796,28Section 2. 20.465 (3) (cm) and (cp) of the statutes are created to read:AB796,2,10920.465 (3) (cm) Grants for security of religious organizations. (1) The division 15shall award grants to nonprofit religious organizations for security purposes. In 16awarding grants, the division shall give priority to applicants that are a target or 17are at risk of being a target of an incident or crime that is motivated, in whole or in 18part, by the actor’s bias against a religion or religious group.AB796,3,219(2) The division shall accept grant applications from any nonprofit religious 20organization, including federations, networks, or other umbrella organizations on 21behalf of a member.
