Under the bill, a “nuclear fusion technology project” is a project that has the purpose of undertaking the controlled fusion of atomic nuclei or research thereupon. The sales and use tax exemption created by the bill sunsets after December 31, 2076.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB657-ASA1,13Section 1. 77.54 (76) of the statutes is created to read:AB657-ASA1,2,4477.54 (76) (a) The sales price from the sale of and the storage, use, or other 5consumption of tangible personal property or property under s. 77.52 (1) (c) 6exclusively and directly used for a nuclear fusion technology project and used solely 1at the nuclear fusion technology project’s location, including purchases of tangible 2personal property or property under s. 77.52 (1) (c) made by construction 3contractors who transfer such property to their customers in fulfillment of a real 4property construction activity.AB657-ASA1,2,95(b) In this subsection, “nuclear fusion technology project” means a project that 6has the purpose of undertaking the controlled fusion of atomic nuclei or research 7thereupon, including energy generation, propulsion systems, materials research, 8medical isotope production, neutron sources, plasma physics research, and any 9other application making use of fusion reactions or fusion-enabling technologies.AB657-ASA1,2,1010(c) This subsection does not apply after December 31, 2076.AB657-ASA1,211Section 2. Effective date.AB657-ASA1,2,1312(1) Nuclear fusion technology project exemption.
