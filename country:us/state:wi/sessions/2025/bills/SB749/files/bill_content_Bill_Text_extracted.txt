Referred to Committee on Licensing, Regulatory Reform, State and Federal Affairs.SB749,1,31An Act to amend 440.88 (4) of the statutes; relating to: substance abuse 2counselor-in-training, clinical supervisor-in-training, and prevention 3specialist-in-training certifications.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauCurrent law provides for the certification of individuals by the Department of Safety and Professional Services as substance abuse counselors, clinical supervisors, or prevention specialists. Individuals may also be certified as any of the aforementioned “in training.” As with other credentials, the renewal date for certifications, including in-training certifications, is a fixed date. In-training certifications may only be renewed twice after initial certification.This bill provides that the renewal date for an in-training certification is two years from the date the certification is granted instead of the fixed date under current law.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB749,14Section 1. 440.88 (4) of the statutes is amended to read:SB749,2,135440.88 (4) Applications; certification period. An application for 1certification as a substance abuse counselor, clinical supervisor, or prevention 2specialist under this section shall be made on a form provided by the department 3and filed with the department and shall be accompanied by the initial credential fee 4determined by the department under s. 440.03 (9) (a). The renewal date for 5certification as a substance abuse counselor, clinical supervisor, or prevention 6specialist is specified under s. 440.08 (2) (a) and the renewal fee for such 7certifications is determined by the department under s. 440.03 (9) (a). 8Notwithstanding s. 440.08 (2) (a) 9m., the renewal date for certification as a 9substance abuse counselor-in-training, a clinical supervisor-in-training, or a 10prevention specialist-in-training is 2 years from the date the certification is 11granted.
