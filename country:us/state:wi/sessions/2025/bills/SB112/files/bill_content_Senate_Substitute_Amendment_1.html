Analysis by the Legislative Reference Bureau
This bill requires the Department of Natural Resources to classify the sandhill crane as a migratory game bird and authorize the hunting of sandhill cranes in this state, subsequent to approval by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service. DNR must establish by rule one sandhill crane hunting season and may establish by rule hunting zones where the hunting of sandhill cranes is permitted. DNR must establish restrictions on sandhill crane hunting permits to minimize the inadvertent taking of other bird species.
The bill requires DNR to issue sandhill crane hunting permits to limit the number of hunters of sandhill cranes and the number of sandhill cranes harvested in any area of the state. The fee for the permit is $10 for residents of this state and $50 for nonresidents. If the number of applications for sandhill crane hunting permits for a given zone or season exceeds the number of available sandhill crane hunting permits, DNR must issue permits according to a cumulative preference system. Under the cumulative preference system, DNR must establish preference categories for those applicants who applied for but were not issued permits, with higher priority for hunting permits issued to those categories with the most preference points versus those with fewer preference points.
The bill requires DNR to establish and conduct a sandhill crane hunter education program. DNR must offer a test for a person to demonstrate successful completion of the course of instruction and shall issue a certificate of accomplishment for free to a person who completes the course of instruction and passes the test. No person may hunt sandhill cranes unless the person has a valid certificate of accomplishment.
