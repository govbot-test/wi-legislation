Under current federal law, a person may deduct certain qualified overtime compensation from the person’s income for federal income tax purposes. Federal law generally defines “qualified overtime compensation” as overtime compensation paid to a person under the federal Fair Labor Standards Act that is in excess of the person’s regular rate of pay. The bill contains no sunset for the subtraction for qualified overtime compensation. Under current federal law, the federal deduction for qualified overtime compensation sunsets after tax year 2028.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB461-ASA3,13Section 1. 71.05 (6) (b) 57. of the statutes is created to read:AB461-ASA3,2,4471.05 (6) (b) 57. a. Subject to subd. 57. b. and c., for taxable years beginning 1after December 31, 2024, the amount that the claimant may deduct under section 2225 of the Internal Revenue Code in effect for federal purposes on the claimant’s 3federal income tax return for the taxable year to which the claim under this 4subdivision relates.AB461-ASA3,2,115b. For an individual who is a part-year resident or nonresident of this state, 6the subtraction under this subdivision for the taxable year may not exceed the 7amount that is calculated by multiplying the amount that the individual may 8deduct under section 225 of the Internal Revenue Code in effect for federal purposes 9on the individual’s federal income tax return by a fraction the numerator of which is 10the individual’s qualified overtime compensation that is taxable by this state and 11the denominator of which is the individual’s total qualified overtime compensation.AB461-ASA3,2,1612c. Notwithstanding 26 USC 225 (g), for taxable years beginning after 13December 31, 2028, a claimant may claim the subtraction under this subdivision as 14if 26 USC 225 (g) did not prohibit the claimant from deducting an amount on the 15claimant’s federal income tax return for taxable years beginning after December 31, 162028.AB461-ASA3,2,1817d. In this subdivision, “qualified overtime compensation” has the meaning 18given in section 225 (c) of the Internal Revenue Code in effect for federal purposes.AB461-ASA3,219Section 2. 71.52 (6) of the statutes is amended to read:AB461-ASA3,4,112071.52 (6) “Income” means the sum of Wisconsin adjusted gross income and 21the following amounts, to the extent not included in Wisconsin adjusted gross 22income: maintenance payments (except foster care maintenance and 23supplementary payments excludable under section 131 of the internal revenue 24code), support money, cash public assistance (not including credit granted under 1this subchapter and amounts under s. 46.27, 2017 stats.), cash benefits paid by 2counties under s. 59.53 (21), the gross amount of any pension or annuity (including 3railroad retirement benefits, all payments received under the federal social security 4act and veterans disability pensions), nontaxable interest received from the federal 5government or any of its instrumentalities, nontaxable interest received on state or 6municipal bonds, worker’s compensation, unemployment insurance, the gross 7amount of “loss of time” insurance, compensation and other cash benefits received 8from the United States for past or present service in the armed forces, scholarship 9and fellowship gifts or income, capital gains, gain on the sale of a personal residence 10excluded under section 121 of the internal revenue code, dividends, income of a 11nonresident or part-year resident who is married to a full-year resident, housing 12allowances provided to members of the clergy, the amount by which a resident 13manager’s rent is reduced, nontaxable income of an American Indian, any amount 14subtracted under s. 71.05 (6) (b) 57., nontaxable income from sources outside this 15state and nontaxable deferred compensation. Intangible drilling costs, depletion 16allowances and depreciation, including first-year depreciation allowances under 17section 179 of the internal revenue code, amortization, contributions to individual 18retirement accounts under section 219 of the internal revenue code, contributions 19to Keogh plans, net operating loss carry-backs and carry-forwards, capital loss 20carry-forwards, and disqualified losses deducted in determining Wisconsin 21adjusted gross income shall be added to “income”. “Income” does not include gifts 22from natural persons, cash reimbursement payments made under title XX of the 23federal social security act, surplus food or other relief in kind supplied by a 24governmental agency, the gain on the sale of a personal residence deferred under 1section 1034 of the internal revenue code or nonrecognized gain from involuntary 2conversions under section 1033 of the internal revenue code. Amounts not included 3in adjusted gross income but added to “income” under this subsection in a previous 4year and repaid may be subtracted from income for the year during which they are 5repaid. Scholarship and fellowship gifts or income that are included in Wisconsin 6adjusted gross income and that were added to household income for purposes of 7determining the credit under this subchapter in a previous year may be subtracted 8from income for the current year in determining the credit under this subchapter. 9A marital property agreement or unilateral statement under ch. 766 has no effect in 10computing “income” for a person whose homestead is not the same as the 11homestead of that person’s spouse.AB461-ASA3,312Section 3. Initial applicability.AB461-ASA3,4,1413(1) Definition of income for homestead credit.
