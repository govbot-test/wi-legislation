relating to: 9the Warren Knowles-Gaylord Nelson Stewardship 2000 Program.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill reauthorizes until 2028, and makes certain changes to, the Warren Knowles-Gaylord Nelson Stewardship 2000 Program.Current law authorizes the state to incur public debt for certain conservation activities under the stewardship program, which is administered by the Department of Natural Resources. The state may incur this debt to acquire land for the state for conservation purposes and for property development activities and may award grants or state aid to certain local governmental units and nonprofit conservation organizations (NCOs) to acquire and develop land for these purposes. Current law establishes the amounts that DNR may obligate in each fiscal year through fiscal year 2025-26 for expenditure under each of three active subprograms of the stewardship program.The bill reauthorizes the stewardship program until fiscal year 2027-28. The bill reduces the amounts DNR may obligate in fiscal years 2026-27 and 2027-28 from $16,000,000 to $1,000,000 under the land acquisition subprogram and from $14,250,000 to $9,250,000 under the property development and local assistance subprogram. The recreational boating aids subprogram obligation limit remains the same at $3,000,000 under the bill.The $1,000,000 that the bill authorizes DNR to obligate under the land acquisition subprogram equals the amount that current law requires to be set aside to be obligated only for DNR to acquire land for the Ice Age Trail.Under current law, in the stewardship program, the term “obligate” means to encumber or otherwise commit or to expend without having previously encumbered or otherwise committed, and the term is used with respect to limits on obligating or requirements to obligate certain amounts in the stewardship program. The bill specifies that “obligate” refers only to encumbering, otherwise committing, or expending public debt that the state is authorized to contract. In other words, “obligate” does not refer to amounts that are not the result of bonding.Under current law, DNR may obligate moneys for local assistance under the subprogram for property development and local assistance only for grant programs for urban green space, local parks, acquisition of property development rights, and urban rivers. Current law requires that such a grant may be for only up to 50 percent of the costs of a project. Under the bill, no more than 30 percent of the remaining costs may be paid with funding provided from grants or in-kind contributions. The bill also provides that if a grant is applied for after closing on the acquisition of the land in question, the grant may be for only up to 40 percent of the acquisition costs.The bill adds to the subprogram for property development and local assistance that DNR may obligate moneys to provide grants to NCOs to develop, manage, preserve, restore, and maintain wildlife habitat on public land to benefit game species and other wildlife, including easements that provide public access for hunting and fishing. Under current law, if DNR does not obligate an amount authorized for a subprogram in a fiscal year, DNR may not raise the annual bonding authority for that subprogram in the next fiscal year. Under current law, portions of the unobligated amounts for the land acquisition, property development and local assistance, and recreational boating aids subprograms from various fiscal years beginning in 2011-12 are obligated for specific purposes. One such provision requires DNR to obligate all unobligated amounts from those subprograms in any fiscal year, including for drilling new wells, facility maintenance, upgrades and renovations, and construction of new buildings. The bill limits this obligation to only those unobligated amounts for those subprograms in fiscal years ending with fiscal year 2025-26.The bill requires DNR, in coordination with counties and NCOs that have at any time received a grant for land acquisition under the stewardship program or the Warren Knowles-Gaylord Nelson Stewardship Program, which was in place from fiscal year 1990-91 to fiscal year 1999-2000 (original stewardship program), to conduct a study and to submit within two years a report to the legislature that includes an inventory of all land acquired with money under the stewardship program or the original stewardship program, proposed project boundaries and land acquisition priorities for the next two to five years, and proposed changes, if any, to the managed forest land program, the forest crop law, and the aids in lieu of taxes program. Under the bill, DNR may submit a request for supplemental funding from the Joint Committee on Finance for the study and report.The bill requires each recipient of a grant under the stewardship program to submit to DNR a report on how the grant was spent. The bill requires DNR to publish, in a manner accessible to the public, each recipient of a grant under the stewardship program and each such report submitted by a grant recipient.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB315-ASA2,11Section 1. 23.09 (19) (d) of the statutes is amended to read:AB315-ASA2,2,6223.09 (19) (d) Except as provided in par.
