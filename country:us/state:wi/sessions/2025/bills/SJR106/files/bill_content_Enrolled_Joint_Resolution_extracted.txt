andWhereas, “Snow Angel” featured music from Disney’s Frozen II, Beyoncé, and Frank Ticheli’s “Angels in the Architecture”; andWhereas, the band also received at the competition awards for Best Music, Best Visuals, and Best Percussion; andWhereas, the band has won the state championship for 20 consecutive years, meaning that the students performing in the band this year were not yet born the last time the Greendale High School Marching Band lost the state championship; andWhereas, in November 2025, the band will perform “Snow Angel” in competition at the Bands of America Grand National Championships in Indianapolis; andWhereas, the Greendale High School Marching Band will be the first high school band ever to perform two times in both the Macy’s Thanksgiving Day Parade and the Tournament of Roses Parade over a 10-year period; andWhereas, nearly 200 students participate in the Greendale High School Marching Band, which constitutes almost 20 percent of the total student body; andWhereas, band director Tom Reifenberg has led the Greendale High School Marching Band since 2001 and teaches a variety of band and music classes at Greendale High School; he is also the cofounder and musical director of the Greendale Community Theatre; andWhereas, Greendale Marching Band students and families volunteer to support local fundraising activities to give back to the community; now, therefore, be itResolved by the senate, the assembly concurring, That the Wisconsin Legislature congratulates the Greendale High School Marching Band on winning first place in Class AA at the Wisconsin School Music Association State Marching Band Championship for the 20th consecutive year.
