These social media platforms must use reasonable means and efforts to estimate the age of an account holder to determine if the account holder is a minor. The bill includes presumptions based on the social media platform’s level of confidence that the account holder is at least 18 years old. These age-estimation requirements do not apply if the account has been held continuously for at least seven years. The social media platform must derive age estimates based on information collected and retained by it in the ordinary course of operation, subject to the limitations described below, and the social media platform is not required to request, collect, or retain information from or about an account holder. However, a social media platform must require that an applicant for an account provide the applicant’s date of birth. If the applicant is a minor, the social media platform may not create or maintain an account for the minor, or change the terms and conditions of the account, unless the social media platform first obtains verifiable parental consent, which is defined in a manner similar to the federal Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act.The bill imposes various requirements and restrictions on an account for a minor, including the following:1. A social media platform must set the default privacy settings for a minor’s account at the most private levels and may not change these settings unless it obtains verifiable parental consent to do so. To obtain verifiable parental consent, the social media platform must provide an option for the parent to receive a separate password allowing the parent to monitor and set controls on the minor’s account.2. A social media platform may not present specified addictive features in the display or feed of a minor, including infinite scrolling; a profile-based feed; push notifications; autoplay video; displaying the number of “likes,” shares, or repostings of the minor’s posted content; or any award or recognition related to the minor’s number of followers or postings or other metrics of usage or performance.3. A social media platform must prevent profile-based, paid commercial advertising in the display or feed of a minor.4. A social media platform must terminate an account if it concludes, or obtains information from which it reasonably should conclude, that the account holder is a minor, unless the social media platform obtains verifiable parental consent for the account. A social media platform must terminate an account of a minor within seven days of receiving a request for termination from the minor or within 14 days of receiving a request for termination from the minor’s parent. The social media platform must also provide clear, simple, and easy-to-locate means for a minor’s parent to request termination of a minor’s account.The bill requires the Department of Agriculture, Trade and Consumer Protection to investigate alleged violations of the bill’s provisions. DATCP, or the Department of Justice or a district attorney in consultation with DATCP, may bring an action to enforce the provisions of the bill and may seek a civil forfeiture of $100 per violation, injunctive relief, an order to pay damages to an injured minor or the minor’s parent, disgorgement of money received by the social media platform, attorney fees, and other costs of investigation and prosecution. The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB963-ASA3,11Section 1. 100.72 of the statutes is created to read:AB963-ASA3,2,32100.72 Social media accounts for minors. (1) Definitions. In this 3section:AB963-ASA3,3,34(a) “Account holder” means an individual who is a resident of this state and 5who has an account or profile with a covered social media platform that is 1associated by the covered social media platform with a unique identifier, during any 2period in which the covered social media platform knows or should reasonably know 3the individual is physically located in this state.AB963-ASA3,3,74(b) “Covered social media platform” means a social media platform and any 5parent, subsidiary, or affiliate of the social media platform that together, in 6aggregate worldwide, generated at least $1,000,000,000 in annual gross revenue in 7at least one of the immediately preceding 3 years.AB963-ASA3,3,88(c) “Infinite scrolling” means any of the following:AB963-ASA3,3,1091. Continuously loading content, or content that loads as the account holder 10scrolls down the page without the need to open a separate page.AB963-ASA3,3,12112. Seamless content, or the use of pages with no visible or apparent end or 12page breaks.AB963-ASA3,3,1413(d) “Minor” means a resident of this state who is less than 18 years of age, 14excluding an emancipated minor, as defined in s. 48.375 (2) (e).AB963-ASA3,3,1515(e) “Parent” includes any legal guardian of a minor.AB963-ASA3,3,1716(f) 1. Except as provided in subd. 2., “personal information” means any of the 17following:AB963-ASA3,3,1918a. Information about an account holder collected online that comprises 19personal information, as defined in 15 USC 6501 (8).AB963-ASA3,3,2220b. Any record of or derived from online activity or history, search history, or 21online communications of an account holder with respect to any website, 22application, or social media platform.AB963-ASA3,4,223c. Any photograph or biometric information that is used or could reasonably 1be used to identify the account holder, including fingerprints, voice prints, iris or 2retina images, facial templates, and gait imagery or metrics.AB963-ASA3,4,43d. Any geolocation information associated with an account holder or with a 4device of an account holder.AB963-ASA3,4,552. “Personal information” does not include any of the following:AB963-ASA3,4,76a. An express search term, request, or selection submitted by the account 7holder during the current session on the covered social media platform.AB963-ASA3,4,98b. An identifier used solely for the purpose of directing personal 9communications to or from the account holder.AB963-ASA3,4,1110c. Information that comprises account-holder-selected or parent-selected 11settings relating to privacy, accessibility, or blocking of age-inappropriate content.AB963-ASA3,4,1212d. Technical information concerning the account holder’s device.AB963-ASA3,4,1613(g) 1. Except as provided in subd. 2., “profile-based feed” means a feed in 14which the material presented has been selected or prioritized by a covered social 15media platform for display to an account holder based in whole or in part on 16personal information of that account holder.AB963-ASA3,4,17172. “Profile-based feed” does not include any of the following:AB963-ASA3,4,2318a. A feed that includes content created by a 3rd party that is displayed to the 19account holder because the account holder has taken an affirmative step to select 20the 3rd-party’s content for inclusion in the feed, such as by “following,” “friending,” 21or engaging in a similar action in relation to the 3rd party, which content is not 22otherwise selected or prioritized for display to the account holder based on personal 23information.AB963-ASA3,5,324b. A feed from which is excluded content based on information about or any 1estimate of the age of the account holder if the content is obscene as to minors or if 2the content is, by policy of the covered social media platform, not suitable for 3presentation to minors.AB963-ASA3,5,84(h) 1. Except as provided in subd. 2., “push notification” means an automatic 5electronic message, whether audible, visual, or tactile, designed to call the attention 6of the account holder to newly posted content, or to user responses to content posted 7by the account holder, or to inform the account holder about other specific activities 8or events related to the account holder’s account.AB963-ASA3,5,1192. “Push notification” does not include a notification alerting the account 10holder of incoming calls, text messages, email messages, or similar messages sent 11by human contacts and delivered by means of any application.AB963-ASA3,5,1512(i) 1. Except as provided in subd. 2., “social media platform” means a website 13or application that is open to the public and that allows a user to create an account 14that enables the user to communicate with other users for the primary purpose of 15posting and viewing information, comments, messages, images, or videos.AB963-ASA3,5,16162. “Social media platform” does not include any of the following:AB963-ASA3,5,1717a. A broadband Internet access service, as defined in 47 CFR 8.1 (b).AB963-ASA3,5,2218b. An online service, website, or application the exclusive function of which is 19the support of communications, including emailing or direct messaging of text, 20images, or videos, between only the sender and recipients specifically identified by 21the sender, without displaying or posting the communications publicly or to other 22users not specifically identified as the recipients by the sender.AB963-ASA3,5,2423c. An online service, website, or application the content of which consists 24primarily of information or content that is not user-generated.AB963-ASA3,6,11d. A peer-to-peer community tech support site.AB963-ASA3,6,92(j) “Verifiable parental consent” means any reasonable effort, taking into 3consideration available technology, to ensure that a parent of a minor receives 4notice of the covered social media platform’s personal information collection, use, 5and disclosure practices and authorizes the collection, use, and disclosure, as 6applicable, of personal information and the subsequent use of that information 7before that information is collected from the minor. “Verifiable parental consent” 8includes a request for authorization for future collection, use, and disclosure of a 9minor’s personal information described in a notice to the minor’s parent.AB963-ASA3,6,1610(2) Age estimation and identification of minors. (a) 1. No later than 14 11days after the date on which an account holder has been on a covered social media 12platform for at least 25 hours within a 6-month period, the covered social media 13platform shall use reasonable means and reasonable efforts, taking into 14consideration available technology and the data in the possession of the covered 15social media platform, to estimate the age of the account holder for purposes of 16subds. 2. and 3.AB963-ASA3,6,20172. If the covered social media platform, in the estimate under subd. 1., is able 18to conclude with 80 percent confidence that the account holder is at least 18 years of 19age, the covered social media platform may treat the account holder as not a minor 20for purposes of this section.AB963-ASA3,6,24213. If the covered social media platform, in the estimate under subd. 1., is not 22able to conclude with 80 percent confidence that the account holder is at least 18 23years of age, the covered social media platform shall treat the account holder as a 24minor for purposes of this section.AB963-ASA3,7,51(b) 1. No later than 14 days after the date on which an account holder has 2been on a covered social media platform for at least 50 hours within a 6-month 3period, the covered social media platform shall use reasonable means and 4reasonable efforts to revise its estimate of the age of the account holder for purposes 5of subds. 2. and 3.AB963-ASA3,7,962. If the covered social media platform, in the revised estimate under subd. 1., 7is able to conclude with 90 percent confidence that the account holder is at least 18 8years of age, the covered social media platform may treat the account holder as not 9a minor for purposes of this section.AB963-ASA3,7,13103. If the covered social media platform, in the revised estimate under subd. 1., 11is not able to conclude with 90 percent confidence that the account holder is at least 1218 years of age, the covered social media platform shall treat the account holder as 13a minor for purposes of this section.AB963-ASA3,7,1914(c) A covered social media platform shall update its estimate of the age of each 15account holder after every additional 100 hours spent by the account holder on the 16covered social media platform or as often as the covered social media platform 17applies any form of data analytics or artificial intelligence to update its estimate of 18any other demographic characteristic of the account holder, whichever period is 19shorter.AB963-ASA3,8,220(d) 1. A covered social media platform shall derive the age estimates required 21under this subsection based on information collected and retained by the covered 22social media platform in the ordinary course of operation of the covered social media 23platform. Nothing in this subsection creates a duty on the part of a covered social 1media platform to request, collect, or retain any information from or about an 2account holder.
