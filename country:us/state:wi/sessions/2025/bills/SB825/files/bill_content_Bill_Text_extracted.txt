Referred to Committee on Transportation and Local Government.SB825,1,41An Act to renumber 13.489 (1c) (a); to amend 13.489 (4) (a) 1. a., 13.489 (4) (a) 21. b. and 13.489 (4m) (a); to create 13.489 (1c) (am) of the statutes; relating 3to: environmental review prerequisites for certain major highway project 4actions.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauUnder current law, the Department of Transportation may recommend highway projects for inclusion in the major highway projects program by submitting a report to the Transportation Projects Commission (TPC) that includes a request for TPC approval to proceed with the project. Under current law, TPC must have been notified that a final environmental impact statement or environmental assessment for the project has been approved by the federal highway administration before it may approve a project under this procedure. This bill changes this requirement to require only that a draft environmental impact statement, environmental assessment, or categorical exclusion have been approved. In addition, the bill specifies that approval of the draft environmental document must be made by DOT unless approval by the federal highway administration is necessary to secure federal funding.Also under current law, DOT may not begin construction of certain major highway projects with a total cost of more than $75,000,000 until a report is provided to TPC. This report may not be submitted by DOT until a draft environmental impact statement or environmental assessment is completed. Under this bill, the report may also be submitted after completion of a draft categorical exclusion.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB825,11Section 1. 13.489 (1c) (a) of the statutes is renumbered 13.489 (1c) (aw).SB825,22Section 2. 13.489 (1c) (am) of the statutes is created to read:SB825,2,5313.489 (1c) (am) “Categorical exclusion” means a categorical exclusion from a 4requirement to prepare an environmental assessment or environmental impact 5statement for a highway project as described under 23 CFR 771.117.SB825,36Section 3. 13.489 (4) (a) 1. a. of the statutes is amended to read:SB825,2,14713.489 (4) (a) 1. a. The commission determines that, within 6 years after the 8first July 1 after the date on which the commission recommends approval of the 9project, construction will be commenced on all projects enumerated under s. 84.013 10(3) and on the project recommended for approval and the commission has been 11notified that a final draft environmental impact statement or, draft environmental 12assessment, or draft categorical exclusion for the project has been approved by the 13department of transportation or, if necessary to secure federal funding, the federal 14highway administration.SB825,415Section 4. 13.489 (4) (a) 1. b. of the statutes is amended to read:SB825,3,41613.489 (4) (a) 1. b. The report recommending approval of the project is 17accompanied by a financing proposal that, if implemented, would provide funding 18in an amount sufficient to ensure that construction will commence on all projects 19enumerated under s. 84.013 (3) and on the project within 6 years after the first July 201 after the date on which the commission recommends approval of the project and 1the commission has been notified that a final draft environmental impact 2statement or, draft environmental assessment, or draft categorical exclusion for the 3project has been approved by the department of transportation or, if necessary to 4secure federal funding, the federal highway administration.SB825,55Section 5. 13.489 (4m) (a) of the statutes is amended to read:SB825,3,12613.489 (4m) (a) Notwithstanding sub. (4), for any major highway project 7described in s. 84.013 (1) (a) 2m., the department of transportation shall submit a 8report to the commission, prior to construction of the project, which report may 9request the commission’s approval to proceed with the project.
