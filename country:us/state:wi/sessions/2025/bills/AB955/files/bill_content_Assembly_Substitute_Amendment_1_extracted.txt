This bill repeals this language and instead provides that no agency may promulgate rules interpreting the provisions of any statute enforced or administered by the agency without explicit and specific statutory authority. The administrative procedure law also has several references to an agency needing explicit rule-making authority in order to promulgate rules. The bill changes these provisions so that they require explicit and specific authority.Current law also provides that no agency may implement or enforce any standard, requirement, or threshold, including as a term or condition of any license issued by the agency, unless that standard, requirement, or threshold is explicitly required or explicitly permitted by statute or by a rule that has been promulgated in accordance with the rule-making process. The bill modifies this provision to require that the standard, requirement, or threshold be explicitly and specifically required or permitted by a statute or rule.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB955-ASA1,11Section 1. 18.03 (2) (b) of the statutes is amended to read:AB955-ASA1,2,5218.03 (2) (b) The commission may interpret the statutes enforced or 3administered by it as it considers to be necessary to effectuate the purpose of the 4statutes. The authority granted by this paragraph shall not exceed the authority 5granted by s. 227.11 (2) (a).AB955-ASA1,26Section 2. 227.10 (2m) of the statutes is amended to read:AB955-ASA1,2,137227.10 (2m) No agency may implement or enforce any standard, requirement, 8or threshold, including as a term or condition of any license issued by the agency, 9unless that standard, requirement, or threshold is explicitly and specifically 10required or explicitly and specifically permitted by statute or by a rule that has 11been promulgated in accordance with this subchapter, except as provided in s. 12186.118 (2) (c) and (3) (b) 3. The governor, by executive order, may prescribe 13guidelines to ensure that rules are promulgated in compliance with this subchapter.AB955-ASA1,314Section 3. 227.11 (1m) of the statutes is created to read:AB955-ASA1,2,1715227.11 (1m) No agency may promulgate rules interpreting the provisions of 16any statute enforced or administered by the agency without explicit and specific 17statutory authority.AB955-ASA1,418Section 4. 227.11 (2) (a) (intro.) of the statutes is repealed.AB955-ASA1,51Section 5. 227.11 (2) (a) 1. to 3. of the statutes are renumbered 227.11 (4) to 2(6), and 227.11 (4) and (5), as renumbered, are amended to read:AB955-ASA1,3,73227.11 (4) A statutory or nonstatutory provision containing a statement or 4declaration of legislative intent, purpose, findings, or policy does not confer rule-5making authority on the agency or augment the agency’s rule-making authority 6beyond the rule-making authority that is explicitly and specifically conferred on the 7agency by the legislature.AB955-ASA1,3,118(5) A statutory provision describing the agency’s general powers or duties 9does not confer rule-making authority on the agency or augment the agency’s rule-10making authority beyond the rule-making authority that is explicitly and 11specifically conferred on the agency by the legislature.AB955-ASA1,612Section 6. 227.11 (2) (d) of the statutes is amended to read:AB955-ASA1,3,1713227.11 (2) (d) An agency may promulgate rules implementing or interpreting 14a statute that it will enforce or administer after publication of the authorizing 15statute but prior to the statute’s effective date. A rule promulgated under this 16paragraph may not take effect prior to the effective date of the authorizing statute 17that it implements or interprets.AB955-ASA1,718Section 7. 227.11 (3) of the statutes is amended to read:AB955-ASA1,3,2419227.11 (3) (a) A plan that is submitted to the federal government for the 20purpose of complying with a requirement of federal law does not confer rule-making 21authority and cannot be used by an agency as authority to promulgate rules. No 22agency may agree to promulgate a rule as a component of a compliance plan unless 23the agency has explicit and specific statutory authority to promulgate the rule at 24the time the compliance plan is submitted.AB955-ASA1,4,61(b) A settlement agreement, consent decree, or court order does not confer 2rule-making authority and cannot be used by an agency as authority to promulgate 3rules. No agency may agree to promulgate a rule as a term in any settlement 4agreement, consent decree, or stipulated order of a court unless the agency has 5explicit and specific statutory authority to promulgate the rule at the time the 6settlement agreement, consent decree, or stipulated order of a court is executed.AB955-ASA1,87Section 8. 227.135 (2) of the statutes is amended to read:AB955-ASA1,5,48227.135 (2) An agency that has prepared a statement of the scope of the 9proposed rule shall present the statement to the department of administration, 10which shall make a determination as to whether the agency has the explicit and 11specific authority to promulgate the rule as proposed in the statement of scope and 12shall report the statement of scope and its determination to the governor who, in his 13or her discretion, may approve or reject the statement of scope. The agency may not 14send the statement to the legislative reference bureau for publication under sub. (3) 15until the governor issues a written notice of approval of the statement. The agency 16shall also present the statement to the individual or body with policy-making 17powers over the subject matter of the proposed rule for approval. The individual or 18body with policy-making powers may not approve the statement until at least 10 19days after publication of the statement under sub. (3) and, if a preliminary public 20hearing and comment period are held by the agency under s. 227.136, until the 21individual or body has received and reviewed any public comments and feedback 22received from the agency under s. 227.136 (5). No state employee or official may 23perform any activity in connection with the drafting of a proposed rule, except for 24an activity necessary to prepare the statement of the scope of the proposed rule 1until the governor and the individual or body with policy-making powers over the 2subject matter of the proposed rule approve the statement. This subsection does 3not prohibit an agency from performing an activity necessary to prepare a petition 4and proposed rule for submission under s. 227.26 (4).AB955-ASA1,5,55(end) Down Down /2025/related/amendments/ab955/asa1_ab955 true amends /2025/related/amendments/ab955/asa1_ab955 amends/2025/REG/AB955-ASA1 amends/2025/REG/AB955-ASA1 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Amendments»AB955»Amendment ASA1-AB955 × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
