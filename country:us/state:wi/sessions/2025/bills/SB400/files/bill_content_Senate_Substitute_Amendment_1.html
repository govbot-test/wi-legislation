SENATE SUBSTITUTE AMENDMENT 1,
TO SENATE BILL 400
November 17, 2025 - Offered by Senator Cabral-Guevara.
SB400-SSA1,1,3
1An Act to create 20.932 of the statutes; relating to: use restrictions on state
2electronic devices and policies and standards concerning certain information
3technology.
Analysis by the Legislative Reference Bureau
Under this bill, no state agency or other body in state government, other than a law enforcement agency for law enforcement purposes, may use an electronic device that is owned, operated, or leased by the state agency to do any of the following:
1. Download, use, or access a web-based or other social media application, channel, or platform that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.
2. Use any user-downloaded software or software application or any generative artificial intelligence that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.
Under the bill a “foreign adversary” is a foreign government or foreign nongovernmental person listed by the U.S. Department of Commerce as a foreign adversary of the United States. Currently, China, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Russia, and Venezuela under the regime of Nicolás Maduro are all deemed foreign adversaries.
Finally, the bill requires the Department of Administration to develop and publish policies and standards for officials, employees, and contractors of executive branch state agencies and authorities, such as the Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation, regarding information technology, as defined by law, owned or controlled by a foreign adversary. The policies and standards must address the information technology’s supply chain, the potential impacts on future procurements, and the potential need for remediation or phase-out of previously procured information technology that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.
The bill requires that the Legislative Technology Services Bureau develop and publish such policies and procedures for the legislature and that the director of state courts do so for the court system.
The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:
