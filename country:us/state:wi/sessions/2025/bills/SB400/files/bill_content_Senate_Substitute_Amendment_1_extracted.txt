Download, use, or access a web-based or other social media application, channel, or platform that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.2. Use any user-downloaded software or software application or any generative artificial intelligence that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.Under the bill a “foreign adversary” is a foreign government or foreign nongovernmental person listed by the U.S. Department of Commerce as a foreign adversary of the United States. Currently, China, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Russia, and Venezuela under the regime of Nicolás Maduro are all deemed foreign adversaries.Finally, the bill requires the Department of Administration to develop and publish policies and standards for officials, employees, and contractors of executive branch state agencies and authorities, such as the Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation, regarding information technology, as defined by law, owned or controlled by a foreign adversary. The policies and standards must address the information technology’s supply chain, the potential impacts on future procurements, and the potential need for remediation or phase-out of previously procured information technology that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.The bill requires that the Legislative Technology Services Bureau develop and publish such policies and procedures for the legislature and that the director of state courts do so for the court system.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB400-SSA1,11Section 1. 20.932 of the statutes is created to read:SB400-SSA1,2,3220.932 Use restrictions of state and local government electronic 3devices. (1) Definitions. In this section:SB400-SSA1,2,74(a) “Artificial intelligence” means an engineered or machine-based system 5that varies in its level of autonomy and that can, for explicit or implicit objectives, 6infer from the input it receives how to generate outputs that can influence physical 7or virtual environments.SB400-SSA1,2,88(b) “Department” means the department of administration.SB400-SSA1,2,109(c) “Foreign adversary” means a foreign government or foreign 10nongovernment person listed as a foreign adversary in 15 CFR 791.4 (a).SB400-SSA1,2,1311(d) “Generative artificial intelligence” means artificial intelligence that can 12generate derived synthetic content, including text, images, video, and audio, that 13emulates the structure and characteristics of its training data.SB400-SSA1,3,214(e) “State agency” means an association, authority, board, department, 15commission, independent agency, institution, system, office, society, or other body 1in state government created or authorized to be created by the constitution or any 2law.SB400-SSA1,3,63(2) Use restrictions. No state agency, other than a law enforcement agency, 4as defined in s. 165.83 (1) (b), for law enforcement purposes, may do any of the 5following using an electronic device that is owned, operated, or leased by the state 6agency:SB400-SSA1,3,87(a) Download, use, or access a web-based or other social media application, 8channel, or platform that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.SB400-SSA1,3,109(b) Use any user-downloaded software, or software application or any 10generative artificial intelligence that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.SB400-SSA1,3,1211(3) Information technology policies and standards. (a) In this 12subsection:SB400-SSA1,3,13131. “Information technology” has the meaning given in s. 16.97 (6).SB400-SSA1,3,15142. “State agency” means a state agency in the executive branch of state 15government and each authority, as defined in s. 16.41 (4).SB400-SSA1,3,2216(b) 1. The department shall develop and publish policies and standards for 17officials, employees, and contractors of state agencies regarding information 18technology that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary. The policies and 19standards shall address the information technology’s supply chain, the potential 20impacts on future state agency procurements, and the potential need for 21remediation or phase-out of previously procured information technology that is 22owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.SB400-SSA1,4,2232. State agencies may not develop or publish their own policies and standards 24regarding information technology that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary 1if the policies and standards are less restrictive than those developed and published 2by the department.SB400-SSA1,4,93(c) The legislative technology services bureau shall develop and publish 4policies and standards for officials, employees, and contractors of the legislature 5regarding information technology that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary. 6The policies and standards shall address the information technology’s supply chain, 7the potential impacts on future procurements, and the potential need for 8remediation or phase-out of previously procured information technology that is 9owned or controlled by a foreign adversary.SB400-SSA1,4,1610(d) The director of state courts shall develop and publish policies and 11standards for officials, employees, and contractors of the court system regarding 12information technology that is owned or controlled by a foreign adversary. The 13policies and standards shall address the information technology’s supply chain, the 14potential impacts on future procurements, and the potential need for remediation 15or phase-out of previously procured information technology that is owned or 16controlled by a foreign adversary.SB400-SSA1,4,1917(e) No policy or standard under par. (b), (c), or (d) applies to a law enforcement 18agency, as defined in s. 165.83 (1) (b), using information technology for law 19enforcement purposes.SB400-SSA1,220Section 2. Effective dates. This act takes effect on January 31, 2027.SB400-SSA1,4,2121(end) Down Down /2025/related/amendments/sb400/ssa1_sb400 true amends /2025/related/amendments/sb400/ssa1_sb400 amends/2025/REG/SB400-SSA1 amends/2025/REG/SB400-SSA1 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Amendments»SB400»Amendment SSA1-SB400 × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
