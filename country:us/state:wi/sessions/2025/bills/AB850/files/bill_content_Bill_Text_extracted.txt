Referred to Committee on Agriculture.AB850,1,21An Act to amend 93.425 (3) of the statutes; relating to: spending cap for the 2Wisconsin agricultural exports program and making an appropriation.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauUnder current law, the Center for International Agribusiness Marketing, operated by the Department of Agriculture, Trade and Consumer Protection, promotes the export of Wisconsin agricultural and agribusiness products in foreign markets. This bill eliminates a spending cap under current law that provides that the center may not expend more than $1,000,000 in any fiscal year. The bill increases the amount appropriated for this purpose to $2,073,400 in fiscal year 2025-26 and $2,097,900 in fiscal year 2026-27. The bill also increases the FTE positions for DATCP by 1.0 GPR position for the purpose of program administration for DATCP’s Center for International Agribusiness Marketing.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB850,13Section 1. 93.425 (3) of the statutes is amended to read:AB850,2,5493.425 (3) Of the moneys appropriated under s. 20.115 (3) (b), the center for 1international agribusiness marketing shall ensure that $2,500,000 is expended for 2the objective specified in sub. (2) (a), $1,250,000 is expended for the objective 3specified in sub. (2) (b), and $1,250,000 is expended for the objective specified in 4sub. (2) (c). The center may not expend more than $1,000,000 under the program in 5any fiscal year.AB850,26Section 2.
