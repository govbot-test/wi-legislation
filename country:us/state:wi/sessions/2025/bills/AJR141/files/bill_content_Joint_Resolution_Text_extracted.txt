Referred to Committee on Rules.AJR141,1,31Relating to: Commemorating the 250th anniversary of one of the first major 2victories for the Patriots in the Revolutionary War and recognizing the 3contributions of Irish Americans in the struggle for independence.AJR141,1,64Whereas, on Saint Patrick’s Day in 1776, the British ended the 11-month siege 5of Boston when they withdrew from the city, leaving it in the control of American 6forces, including General John Sullivan, the son of Irish immigrants; andAJR141,1,97Whereas, General John Sullivan, who was named “Officer of the Day” by 8George Washington, went on to be the governor of New Hampshire, and his brother 9James later served as the governor of Massachusetts; andAJR141,1,1410Whereas, we celebrate the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of 11Independence and the eight Irish American signatories thereof, three of whom were 12born in Ireland: Thomas McKean, who went on to serve as president of the 13Continental Congress; Charles Carroll; James Smith; George Taylor; Matthew 14Thornton; Edward Rutledge; Thomas Lynch Jr.; and George Read; andAJR141,2,31Whereas, these eight men came from different communities and had varied 2upbringings as the sons of lawyers, clergymen, farmers, and indentured laborers, 3yet they stood united in their efforts to create a republic in the United States; andAJR141,2,74Whereas, the spirit of independence, Enlightenment-era republican ideas, 5and Irish defiance of these eight men are at the heart of the American and Irish 6republics they crucially helped found, with their work inspiring generations in 7Ireland to seek and ultimately secure an Irish republic; andAJR141,2,118Whereas, the first 200 copies of the Declaration of Independence were printed 9by John Dunlap, who emigrated to the United States from County Tyrone in 10Northern Ireland, and its first international appearance was in Ireland’s Belfast 11Newsletter; andAJR141,2,1412Whereas, 250 years ago, Stephen Moylan, George Washington’s Irish 13American aide-de-camp, was the first to commit the phrase “United States of 14America” to paper; andAJR141,2,1915Whereas, in 1784, Lord Mountjoy reported to the British Parliament that 16“America was lost by Irish emigrants . . . I am assured from the best authority, the 17major part of the American Army was composed of Irish and that the Irish language 18was as commonly spoken in the American ranks as English. I am also informed it 19was their valor that determined the contest;” now, therefore, be itAJR141,2,2220Resolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the legislature 21commemorates the anniversaries of these events and the contributions of Irish 22American Patriots in the struggle for independence; and, be it furtherAJR141,3,223Resolved, That the chief clerk of the assembly shall provide copies of this 1resolution to Senator Mark Daly, chair of the Senate of Ireland, and the national co-2chairs of the American Irish State Legislators Caucus.AJR141,3,33(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/ajr141 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/ajr141 proposaltext/2025/REG/AJR141 proposaltext/2025/REG/AJR141 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»AJR141: Joint Resolution Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
