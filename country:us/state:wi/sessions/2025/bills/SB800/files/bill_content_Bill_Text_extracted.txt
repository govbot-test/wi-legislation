Referred to Committee on Transportation and Local Government.SB800,1,21An Act to create 59.42 (4) of the statutes; relating to: office of county board 2counsel in certain counties.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill creates the office of board counsel in any county that employs a corporation counsel and that has a county executive or county administrator. Board counsel is appointed by the county board and serves at the pleasure of the county board. Board counsel is independent of the corporation counsel and provides advice and legal service to the county board.For further information see the local fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB800,13Section 1. 59.42 (4) of the statutes is created to read:SB800,2,5459.42 (4) Board counsel. In any county that employs a corporation counsel, 5including a corporation counsel jointly designated by 2 or more counties, and that 6has a county executive or county administrator, there is created, independent of the 1corporation counsel, the office of board counsel. Board counsel is appointed by the 2county board and serves at the pleasure of the county board. Board counsel and any 3deputy board counsels shall be attorneys at law licensed in this state. The duties of 4board counsel are to provide advice and legal service to the county board. The 5board may leave the office of board counsel unfilled at the pleasure of the board.SB800,2,66(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/sb800 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/sb800 proposaltext/2025/REG/SB800 proposaltext/2025/REG/SB800 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»SB800: Bill Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
