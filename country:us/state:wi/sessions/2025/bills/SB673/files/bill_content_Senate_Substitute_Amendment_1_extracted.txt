relating to: requiring public and 2private schools to adopt policies and provide annual trainings on appropriate 3communication between school employees and pupils.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB673-SSA1,14Section 1. 118.07 (7) of the statutes is created to read:SB673-SSA1,2,95118.07 (7) (a) By September 1, 2026, each school board, governing body of a 6private school, and governing board of a charter school established under s. 118.40 7(2r) or (2x) shall adopt a policy on appropriate communication between an employee 8or volunteer, when acting in the individual’s official capacity, and pupils enrolled in 9the applicable school district, private school, or charter school; pupils attending a 10school that is not under the control of the applicable school board, governing body, 1or governing board; and pupils attending a home-based private educational 2program. The school board, governing body of the private school, or governing 3board of the charter school established under s. 118.40 (2r) or (2x) shall include in 4the policy a range of consequences, the most serious of which is termination, for 5employees and volunteers who violate the policy. The school board, governing body 6of the private school, or governing board of the charter school established under s. 7118.40 (2r) or (2x) shall specify that the policy applies to communications that occur 8during or outside of school hours and shall include in the policy standards for all of 9the following:SB673-SSA1,2,10101. Appropriate content.SB673-SSA1,2,11112. Appropriate methods of communication.SB673-SSA1,2,2312(b) Annually, beginning in the 2026-27 school year, each school board, 13governing body of a private school, and governing board of a charter school 14established under s. 118.40 (2r) or (2x) shall provide to its employees training in 15identifying, preventing, and reporting grooming and professional boundary 16violations. The department shall develop and make available to public and private 17schools, at no cost, a training in identifying, preventing, and reporting grooming 18and professional boundary violations. A school board, governing body of a private 19school, or governing board of a charter school established under s. 118.40 (2r) or (2x) 20may provide to its employees the training developed by the department under this 21paragraph or a different training that the school board, governing body of a private 22school, or governing board of a charter school determines is sufficient to ensure 23compliance with its policy under par.
