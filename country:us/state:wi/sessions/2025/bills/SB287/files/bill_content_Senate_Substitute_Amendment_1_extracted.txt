The E-Verify Program is a system operated by the federal Department of Homeland Security and the federal Social Security Administration that allows an employer to enroll in the system and verify that its employees are eligible to work in the United States. The bill provides that the requirement applies to subcontractors as well as contractors.State government and local government employment practicesThis bill provides that a state government agency or local governmental unit that intends to hire an employee after the bill’s effective date must verify the individual’s identity and eligibility to work in the United States under the E-Verify Program. A state government agency or local governmental unit may not offer employment to any individual who is identified under the E-Verify Program as ineligible to work in the United States.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB287-SSA1,11Section 1. 16.856 of the statutes is created to read:SB287-SSA1,2,3216.856 Employment eligibility verification requirement. (1) In this 3section:SB287-SSA1,2,44(a) “Contractual services” has the meaning given in s. 16.70 (3).SB287-SSA1,2,85(b) “Federal employment eligibility verification program” means the system 6operated by the federal department of homeland security and the federal social 7security administration that allows an employer to enroll in the system and verify 8that its employees are eligible to work in the United States.SB287-SSA1,2,129(2) Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, the department may 10not let any construction work or any contract for the purchase of materials, 11supplies, equipment, or contractual services with an estimated cost of $50,000 or 12more by contract under this chapter to any of the following:SB287-SSA1,2,1513(a) Except as provided in sub. (5), an employer that is not enrolled in the 14federal employment eligibility verification program and that has not verified upon 15hire the eligibility of each of its employees to work in the United States.SB287-SSA1,2,1816(b) An employer that knowingly employs an individual who has been 17identified by the federal employment eligibility verification program as ineligible to 18work in the United States.SB287-SSA1,3,519(c) If the department, a designated purchasing agent under s. 16.71, an entity 1or authorized individual under s. 16.74, or any authority, as defined in s. 16.70 (2), 2discovers that it has entered into a contract that violates par. (b), it shall terminate 3the contract without liability for the uncompleted portion of the contract or any 4materials or services purchased or paid for by the employer for use in completing 5the contract.SB287-SSA1,3,76(3) A subcontractor shall comply with the provisions of this section in the 7same manner as a contractor.SB287-SSA1,3,118(4) All contracts subject to this section must contain written notification of 9the requirements in this section and a place for the employer that is awarded the 10contract to sign indicating that the employer is in compliance with the 11requirements.SB287-SSA1,3,1312(5) This section does not apply if the federal employment eligibility 13verification program is discontinued.SB287-SSA1,214Section 2. 66.05025 of the statutes is created to read:SB287-SSA1,3,161566.05025 Employment eligibility verification. (1) Definitions. In this 16section:SB287-SSA1,3,1817(a) “Federal employment eligibility verification program” has the meaning 18given in s. 16.856 (1) (b).SB287-SSA1,3,2019(b) “Local governmental unit” has the meaning given for “municipality” in s. 2016.70 (8).SB287-SSA1,4,421(2) Verification requirements. A local governmental unit that intends to 22hire an employee on or after the effective date of this subsection .... [LRB inserts 23date], shall verify under the federal employment eligibility verification program the 1identity of the individual and the eligibility of that individual to work in the United 2States. A local governmental unit may not offer employment to any individual who 3is identified under the federal employment eligibility verification program as 4ineligible to work in the United States.SB287-SSA1,4,65(3) Termination of program. This section does not apply if the federal 6employment eligibility verification program is discontinued.SB287-SSA1,37Section 3. 230.144 of the statutes is created to read:SB287-SSA1,4,98230.144 Employment eligibility verification program. (1) In this 9section:SB287-SSA1,4,1110(a) “Agency” has the meaning given in s. 230.03 (3), except that it includes the 11Board of Regents of the University of Wisconsin System. SB287-SSA1,4,1312(b) “Federal employment eligibility verification program” has the meaning 13given in s. 16.856 (1) (b).SB287-SSA1,4,1914(2) Beginning on the effective date of this subsection .... [LRB inserts date], an 15agency shall verify under the federal employment eligibility verification program 16the identity of each individual the agency intends to appoint to a position and the 17eligibility of that individual to work in the United States. An agency may not offer 18employment to any individual who is identified under the federal employment 19eligibility verification program as ineligible to work in the United States.SB287-SSA1,4,2120(3) This section does not apply if the federal employment eligibility 21verification program is discontinued.SB287-SSA1,422Section 4. Initial applicability.SB287-SSA1,5,223(1) State procurement and construction contracts.
