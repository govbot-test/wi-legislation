Referred to Committee on Local Government.AB1194,1,21An Act to amend 125.06 (6) and 125.09 (6) of the statutes; relating to: the 2sale of alcohol beverages by a municipality or county in a park.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill allows a municipality or county to sell wine and distilled spirits in its public parks without an alcohol beverage license.Under current law, with limited exceptions, no person may sell alcohol beverages to a consumer unless the seller possesses a license or permit authorizing the sale. Under one exception, no license or permit is required for the sale, by officers or employees of a county or municipality, of fermented malt beverages (beer) in a public park operated by the county or municipality.The bill applies this exception to wine and distilled spirits, along with beer.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB1194,13Section 1. 125.06 (6) of the statutes is amended to read:AB1194,2,24125.06 (6) Public parks. The sale of fermented malt alcohol beverages in any 5public park operated by a county or municipality. Fermented malt Alcohol 1beverages shall be sold by officers or employees of the county or municipality under 2an ordinance, resolution, rule, or regulation enacted by the governing body.AB1194,23Section 2. 125.09 (6) of the statutes is amended to read:AB1194,2,74125.09 (6) Municipal stores sales.
