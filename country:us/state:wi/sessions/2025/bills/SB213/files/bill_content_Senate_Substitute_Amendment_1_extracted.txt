relating 5to: a rail infrastructure modernization credit and a rail infrastructure 6maintenance credit.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB213-SSA1,17Section 1. 71.05 (6) (a) 15. of the statutes, as affected by 2025 Wisconsin Act 815, is amended to read:SB213-SSA1,2,4971.05 (6) (a) 15. The amount of the credits computed under s. 71.07 (2dm), 10(2dx), (2dy), (3g), (3h), (3n), (3q), (3s), (3t), (3w), (3wm), (3y), (4k), (4n), (5f), (5h), 1(5i), (5j), (5k), (5r), (5rm), (6n), (8t), (8v), and (10) and not passed through by a 2partnership, limited liability company, or tax-option corporation that has added 3that amount to the partnership’s, company’s, or tax-option corporation’s income 4under s. 71.21 (4) or 71.34 (1k) (g).SB213-SSA1,25Section 2. 71.07 (8t) of the statutes is created to read:SB213-SSA1,2,7671.07 (8t) Rail infrastructure modernization credit. (a) Definitions. In 7this subsection:SB213-SSA1,2,981. “Claimant” means a person certified to receive tax benefits under s. 9238.309 (2) (a).SB213-SSA1,2,16102. “Qualified new rail infrastructure expenditures” means capital 11expenditures for rail infrastructure and improvements in this state placed in 12service after December 31, 2025, including expenditures for the acquisition of right-13of-way; engineering; construction of new track such as industrial leads, switches, 14spurs, and sidings; rehabilitation of existing inactive track to reinstate operation; 15loading dock improvements; and transloading structures involved with servicing 16customer locations or expansions.SB213-SSA1,2,2217(b) Filing claims. For taxable years beginning after December 31, 2025, and 18before January 1, 2036, and subject to the limitations provided in this subsection, a 19claimant may claim as a credit against the tax imposed under s. 71.02, up to the 20amount of those taxes, an amount equal to 50 percent of the qualified new rail 21infrastructure expenditures made by the claimant during the taxable year to which 22the claim relates.SB213-SSA1,2,2423(c) Limitations. 1. No claimant may use an expenditure to claim both a credit 24under this subsection and a credit under sub. (8v).SB213-SSA1,3,312. The total amount of the credits under this subsection and ss. 71.28 (8t) and 271.47 (8t) for a claimant for a taxable year may not exceed $2,000,000 per credit 3application approved and verified under s. 238.309.SB213-SSA1,3,643. No credit may be allowed under this subsection unless the claimant 5includes with the claimant’s return a copy of the claimant’s verification under s. 6238.309.SB213-SSA1,3,1474. Partnerships, tax-option corporations, and limited liability companies may 8not claim a credit under this subsection, but the eligibility for, and the amount of, 9the credit are based on their expenditures made under par. (b). A partnership, tax-10option corporation, or limited liability company shall compute the amount of the 11credit that each of its partners, shareholders, or members may claim and shall 12provide that information to each of them. Partners of a partnership, shareholders 13of tax-option corporations, and members of limited liability companies may claim 14the credit in proportion to their ownership interest.SB213-SSA1,3,1715(d) Administration. 1. Section 71.28 (4) (e), (g), and (h), as it applies to the 16credit under s. 71.28 (4), applies to the credit under this subsection, including 17credits transferred under par. (e).SB213-SSA1,3,23182. If a credit computed under this subsection is not entirely offset against 19Wisconsin income or franchise taxes otherwise due, the unused balance may be 20carried forward and credited against Wisconsin income or franchise taxes 21otherwise due for the following 5 taxable years to the extent not offset by these 22taxes otherwise due in all intervening years between the year in which the 23expenditure was made and the year in which the carry-forward credit is claimed.SB213-SSA1,4,924(e) Transfer. 1. Any person may sell or otherwise transfer a credit claimed 1under par. (b), in whole or in part, to another person who is subject to the taxes 2imposed under s. 71.02, 71.23, or 71.43, if the person notifies the department of the 3transfer, and submits with the notification a copy of the transfer documents, and 4the department approves the transfer. The transferor may file a claim for a credit 5under par. (b) on a form prescribed by the department at the time of the transfer 6request. Subject to subd. 2. b., the transferee may first use the credit to offset tax in 7the taxable year of the transferor in which the transfer occurs, and may use the 8credit only to offset tax in taxable years otherwise allowed to be claimed and carried 9forward by the original claimant.SB213-SSA1,4,16102. a. If a person’s certification under s. 238.309 (2) (a) is revoked by the 11Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation and the person used the 12certification to claim a credit under this subsection and transferred the credit under 13subd. 1., the person shall add to the person’s liability for taxes imposed under s. 1471.02 the total amount of the credits that were credited against Wisconsin income 15or franchise taxes by the person or by any person to whom the credits were 16transferred.SB213-SSA1,4,2117b. If a person’s certification under s. 238.309 (2) (a) is revoked by the 18Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation and the person used the 19certification to claim a credit under this subsection and transferred the credit under 20subd. 1., the transferee may not use the credit to offset Wisconsin income or 21franchise taxes.SB213-SSA1,322Section 3. 71.07 (8v) of the statutes is created to read:SB213-SSA1,4,242371.07 (8v) Rail infrastructure maintenance credit. (a) Definitions. In 24this subsection:SB213-SSA1,5,211. “Claimant” means a person certified to receive tax benefits under s. 2238.309 (2) (b).SB213-SSA1,5,432. “Qualified short line railroad maintenance expenditures” means all of the 4following:SB213-SSA1,5,85a. Gross expenditures for railroad infrastructure rehabilitation or 6maintenance improvements located in this state, including rail, tie plates, joint 7bars, fasteners, switches, ballast, subgrade, roadbed, industrial leads, sidings, 8signs, safety barriers, crossing signals and gates, and related track structures.SB213-SSA1,5,109b. Gross expenditures for 3rd-party labor related to expenditures described in 10subd. 2. a.SB213-SSA1,5,1211c. Gross expenditures for wages paid to employees in positions directly related 12to maintenance activities for expenditures described in subd. 2. a.SB213-SSA1,5,1813(b) Filing claims. For taxable years beginning after December 31, 2025, and 14before January 1, 2036, and subject to the limitations provided in this subsection, a 15claimant may claim as a credit against the tax imposed under s. 71.02, up to the 16amount of those taxes, an amount equal to 50 percent of the qualified short line 17railroad maintenance expenditures made by the claimant during the taxable year 18to which the claim relates.SB213-SSA1,5,2119(c) Limitations. 1. No credit may be claimed under this subsection for any 20qualified short line railroad maintenance expenditures that are used to claim a tax 21credit under federal law or that are funded by a federal or state grant.SB213-SSA1,5,23222. No claimant may use an expenditure to claim both a credit under this 23subsection and a credit under sub. (8t).SB213-SSA1,6,4243. The total amount of the credits under this subsection and ss. 71.28 (8v) and 171.47 (8v) for a claimant for a taxable year may not exceed an amount equal to 2$5,000 multiplied by the number of miles of railroad track owned or leased by the 3claimant in this state on December 31 of the taxable year to which the claim 4applies.SB213-SSA1,6,754. No credit may be allowed under this subsection unless the claimant 6submits an application under s. 238.309 and includes with the claimant’s return a 7copy of the claimant’s verification under s. 238.309.SB213-SSA1,6,1585. Partnerships, tax-option corporations, and limited liability companies may 9not claim a credit under this subsection, but the eligibility for, and the amount of, 10the credit are based on their expenditures made under par. (b). A partnership, tax-11option corporation, or limited liability company shall compute the amount of the 12credit that each of its partners, shareholders, or members may claim and shall 13provide that information to each of them. Partners of a partnership, shareholders 14of tax-option corporations, and members of limited liability companies may claim 15the credit in proportion to their ownership interest.SB213-SSA1,6,1816(d) Administration. 1.
