Referred to Committee on Rules.AR16,1,11Relating to: proclaiming May 2026 as Lyme Disease Awareness Month.AR16,1,32Whereas, Lyme disease is a bacterial disease transmitted to humans by an 3infected tick commonly known as the blacklegged or deer tick; andAR16,1,64Whereas, according to the Wisconsin Department of Health Services (DHS), 5the disease was first recognized in the United States in 1975 in Lyme, Connecticut; 6andAR16,1,97Whereas, Lyme disease is the most commonly reported vector-borne disease in 8the United States, with an estimated 476,000 Americans diagnosed each year, and 9it is the highest reported tick-borne disease in Wisconsin; andAR16,1,1210Whereas, signs and symptoms generally set in 3 to 30 days after tick bite and 11commonly include fever, headache, fatigue, and circular skin rash called erythema 12migrans (EM); andAR16,2,213Whereas, if left untreated, the infection may spread to other parts of the body, 14producing symptoms that include additional EM lesions, facial or Bell’s palsy, 1severe headaches and neck stiffness, pain and swelling in large joints, cognitive 2decline, shooting pains, and heart palpitations; andAR16,2,53Whereas, although Lyme disease is often difficult to diagnose, the disease can 4be treated successfully with oral antibiotics or injectable antibiotics if it is detected 5early; andAR16,2,96Whereas, the best ways to prevent Lyme disease are to avoid wooded and 7bushy areas with high grass, use repellants with 20 to 30 percent DEET, wear light-8colored clothing to cover exposed skin, and check skin thoroughly after being in 9areas where ticks may be present; andAR16,2,1210Whereas, according to DHS, reported Lyme disease cases have quadrupled 11over the past 20 years, with Wisconsin experiencing 6,469 cases of Lyme disease in 122024, the highest on record; andAR16,2,1613Whereas, it is important to provide information and raise public awareness of 14Lyme disease causes, effects, and treatments and to emphasize important 15education and research efforts surrounding Lyme disease and other tick-borne 16diseases; now, therefore, be itAR16,2,1917Resolved by the assembly, That the Wisconsin Assembly recognizes May 182026 as Lyme Disease Awareness Month in Wisconsin and commends this 19observance to all residents; and, be it furtherAR16,2,2320Resolved, That the Wisconsin Assembly calls upon all residents to learn 21about the importance of tick avoidance and personal protection, tick detection and 22removal, the signs and symptoms of tick-borne diseases, and the need for prompt 23diagnosis and treatment of Lyme disease.AR16,2,2424(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/ar16 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/ar16 proposaltext/2025/REG/AR16 proposaltext/2025/REG/AR16 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»AR16: Resolution Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
