Under current federal law, a person may deduct certain qualified tips from the person’s income for federal income tax purposes. Federal law generally defines “qualified tips” as cash or charged tips received by an individual in an occupation that traditionally and customarily received tips on or before December 31, 2024, as determined by the secretary of the U.S. Department of the Treasury.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB38-ASA2,13Section 1. 71.05 (6) (b) 57. of the statutes is created to read:AB38-ASA2,2,3471.05 (6) (b) 57. a. Subject to the limitation under subd. 57. b., for taxable 5years beginning after December 31, 2024, and before January 1, 2029, the amount 1that the claimant may deduct under section 224 of the Internal Revenue Code in 2effect for federal purposes on the claimant’s federal income tax return for the 3taxable year to which the claim under this subdivision relates.AB38-ASA2,2,104b. For an individual who is a part-year resident or nonresident of this state, 5the subtraction under this subdivision for the taxable year may not exceed the 6amount that is calculated by multiplying the amount that the individual may 7deduct under section 224 of the Internal Revenue Code in effect for federal purposes 8on the individual’s federal income tax return by a fraction the numerator of which is 9the individual’s qualified tips that are taxable by this state and the denominator of 10which is the individual’s total qualified tips.AB38-ASA2,2,1211c. In this subdivision, “qualified tips” has the meaning given in section 224 (d) 12of the Internal Revenue Code in effect for federal purposes.AB38-ASA2,213Section 2. 71.52 (6) of the statutes is amended to read:AB38-ASA2,4,51471.52 (6) “Income” means the sum of Wisconsin adjusted gross income and 15the following amounts, to the extent not included in Wisconsin adjusted gross 16income: maintenance payments (except foster care maintenance and 17supplementary payments excludable under section 131 of the internal revenue 18code), support money, cash public assistance (not including credit granted under 19this subchapter and amounts under s. 46.27, 2017 stats.), cash benefits paid by 20counties under s. 59.53 (21), the gross amount of any pension or annuity (including 21railroad retirement benefits, all payments received under the federal social security 22act and veterans disability pensions), nontaxable interest received from the federal 23government or any of its instrumentalities, nontaxable interest received on state or 24municipal bonds, worker’s compensation, unemployment insurance, the gross 1amount of “loss of time” insurance, compensation and other cash benefits received 2from the United States for past or present service in the armed forces, scholarship 3and fellowship gifts or income, capital gains, gain on the sale of a personal residence 4excluded under section 121 of the internal revenue code, dividends, income of a 5nonresident or part-year resident who is married to a full-year resident, housing 6allowances provided to members of the clergy, the amount by which a resident 7manager’s rent is reduced, nontaxable income of an American Indian, any amount 8subtracted under s. 71.05 (6) (b) 57., nontaxable income from sources outside this 9state and nontaxable deferred compensation. Intangible drilling costs, depletion 10allowances and depreciation, including first-year depreciation allowances under 11section 179 of the internal revenue code, amortization, contributions to individual 12retirement accounts under section 219 of the internal revenue code, contributions 13to Keogh plans, net operating loss carry-backs and carry-forwards, capital loss 14carry-forwards, and disqualified losses deducted in determining Wisconsin 15adjusted gross income shall be added to “income”. “Income” does not include gifts 16from natural persons, cash reimbursement payments made under title XX of the 17federal social security act, surplus food or other relief in kind supplied by a 18governmental agency, the gain on the sale of a personal residence deferred under 19section 1034 of the internal revenue code or nonrecognized gain from involuntary 20conversions under section 1033 of the internal revenue code. Amounts not included 21in adjusted gross income but added to “income” under this subsection in a previous 22year and repaid may be subtracted from income for the year during which they are 23repaid. Scholarship and fellowship gifts or income that are included in Wisconsin 24adjusted gross income and that were added to household income for purposes of 1determining the credit under this subchapter in a previous year may be subtracted 2from income for the current year in determining the credit under this subchapter. 3A marital property agreement or unilateral statement under ch. 766 has no effect in 4computing “income” for a person whose homestead is not the same as the 5homestead of that person’s spouse.AB38-ASA2,36Section 3. Initial applicability.AB38-ASA2,4,87(1) Definition of income for homestead credit.
