relating to: immunity from prosecution for certain crimes based 5on assisting a victim of sexual assault, extending the time limit for 6prosecution of second-degree sexual assault, and the standard for terminating 7residential residency when tenant is the victim of sexual assault.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB413-SSA1,18Section 1. 125.07 (5) (a) 1. and 2. of the statutes are amended to read:SB413-SSA1,2,29125.07 (5) (a) 1. “Bystander” means a person who is present with a crime 1victim at the time of or immediately following the alleged crime or is a witness, as 2defined in s. 940.41 (3), to the alleged crime.SB413-SSA1,2,432. “Crime victim” means a person who claims to have been the victim of a 4crime constituting.SB413-SSA1,2,751m. “Crime” means a violation under s. 940.22 (2), 940.225, 940.302, 948.02 6(1) or (2), 948.025, or 948.05 to 948.095 or 944.06, or a crime under ch. 948 that 7involves sexual contact or sexually explicit conduct.SB413-SSA1,28Section 2. 125.07 (5) (b) of the statutes is amended to read:SB413-SSA1,2,139125.07 (5) (b) Subject to par. (c), an underage person may not be issued a 10citation for, or convicted of, a violation of sub. (4) (a) or (b), nor may an underage 11person’s pretrial release, probation, extended supervision, parole, or supervised 12release be revoked based on a violation of sub. (4) (a) or (b), if all of the following 13apply:SB413-SSA1,2,21141. The underage person is a crime victim or bystander and either the crime 15victim or the bystander requested emergency assistance in connection with the 16alleged crime, by dialing the telephone number “911” or by other means, in 17connection with the alleged crime or the underage person encountered a law 18enforcement officer at a medical facility at which the time that the crime victim 19received treatment in connection with the alleged crime, or the underage person 20reported or disclosed the alleged crime with the intent to obtain assistance for the 21crime.SB413-SSA1,3,6222. The underage person remains at the scene until emergency assistance 23arrives and thereafter cooperates with providers of emergency assistance, including 24furnishing any all requested information, unless the underage person lacks 1capacity to cooperate when emergency medical assistance arrives. If the underage 2person encounters a law enforcement officer at a medical facility as a result of 3reporting or disclosing the alleged crime with the intent to obtain assistance for the 4crime, the underage person cooperates with the officer and furnishes any all 5requested information, unless the underage person lacks capacity to cooperate with 6the officer.SB413-SSA1,37Section 3. 125.07 (5) (c) of the statutes is amended to read:SB413-SSA1,3,118125.07 (5) (c) Paragraph (b) does not apply to an underage person who 9requests emergency assistance, by dialing the telephone number “911” or by other 10means, with an intention to claim the protections under par. (b) and knowing that 11the fact situation that he or she reports does not exist.SB413-SSA1,412Section 4. 165.775 (4) (a) of the statutes is amended to read:SB413-SSA1,3,1713165.775 (4) (a) If the state crime laboratories takes possession of a sexual 14assault kit and the victim chooses not to report the sexual assault to a Wisconsin 15law enforcement agency, the state crime laboratories shall securely store the sexual 16assault kit for a period of 10 20 years, during which time the sexual assault victim 17may choose to report the assault to a Wisconsin law enforcement agency.SB413-SSA1,518Section 5. 704.16 (1) (b) 3. of the statutes is amended to read:SB413-SSA1,3,2219704.16 (1) (b) 3. An injunction order under s. 813.125 (4) protecting the tenant 20or a child of the tenant from the another person, based on the other person’s 21engaging in an act that would constitute sexual assault under s. 940.225, 948.02, or 22948.025, or stalking under s. 940.32, or attempting or threatening to do the same.SB413-SSA1,623Section 6. 704.16 (1) (b) 5. of the statutes is renumbered 704.16 (1m) (b).SB413-SSA1,724Section 7. 704.16 (1m) of the statutes is created to read:SB413-SSA1,4,41704.16 (1m) Terminating tenancy by tenant: sexual assault. A 2residential tenant may terminate his or her tenancy and remove from the premises 3if the tenant provides the landlord with notice in the manner provided under s. 4704.21 and with a certified copy of any of the following:SB413-SSA1,4,85(a) An injunction order under s. 813.125 (4) protecting the tenant or a child of 6the tenant from another person, based on the other person’s engaging in an act that 7would constitute sexual assault under s. 940.225, 948.02, or 948.025, or attempting 8or threatening to do the same.SB413-SSA1,89Section 8. 704.16 (2m) of the statutes is created to read:SB413-SSA1,4,1610704.16 (2m) Not liable for rent: sexual assault. If a residential tenant 11removes from the premises and provides the landlord with a certified copy specified 12under sub. (1m) and with notice that complies with s. 704.21, the tenant shall not be 13liable for any rent after the end of the month following the month in which he or she 14provides the notice or removes from the premises, whichever is later. The tenant’s 15liability for rent under this subsection is subject to the landlord’s duty to mitigate 16damages as provided in s. 704.29 (2).SB413-SSA1,917Section 9. 704.16 (4) (a) of the statutes is amended to read:SB413-SSA1,4,2118704.16 (4) (a) Subject to pars. (b) and (c), regardless of whether sub. (1) or 19(1m) applies, at the request of a residential tenant who provides the landlord with 20a certified copy of a document specified in sub. (1) (b) 1. to 7. or (1m) (a) or (b), a 21landlord shall change the locks to the tenant’s premises.SB413-SSA1,1022Section 10. 704.16 (4) (c) 1. a. of the statutes is amended to read:SB413-SSA1,5,223704.16 (4) (c) 1. a. A document specified in sub. (1) (b) 1., 2., or 3. or (1m) (a) 1that directs the tenant who is the subject of the document to avoid the residence of 2the tenant requesting that the locks be changed.SB413-SSA1,113Section 11. 939.74 (2) (ar) of the statutes is amended to read:SB413-SSA1,5,74939.74 (2) (ar) A prosecution for a violation of s. 940.225 (2) or may be 5commenced within 20 years after the commission of the violation. A prosecution for 6a violation of s. 940.225 (3) may be commenced within 10 years after the 7commission of the violation.SB413-SSA1,128Section 12. 961.444 of the statutes is created to read:SB413-SSA1,5,129961.444 Person seeking assistance following sexual assault; 10immunity from prosecution. (1) Definition. In this section, “sexual assault” 11means conduct that is in violation of s. 940.22 (2), 940.225, 940.302, or 944.06 or a 12crime under ch. 948 that involves sexual contact or sexually explicit conduct.SB413-SSA1,5,1713(2) Immunity for aider. A person is immune from being charged or 14prosecuted under s. 961.573 for the possession of drug paraphernalia, under s. 15961.41 (3g) for the possession of a controlled substance or a controlled substance 16analog, and under s. 961.69 (2) for possession of a masking agent if all of the 17following apply:SB413-SSA1,5,2318(a) The person requested emergency assistance in connection with a sexual 19assault by calling the telephone number “911” or by other means, the person 20encountered a law enforcement officer at a medical facility at the time that the 21victim of the sexual assault received treatment in connection with the sexual 22assault, or the person reported or disclosed the sexual assault with the intent to 23obtain assistance for the sexual assault.SB413-SSA1,6,21(b) The evidence for the charge or prosecution is obtained as a result of the 2person’s action described under par. (a).SB413-SSA1,6,43(c) The person provides a name and contact information and furnishes all 4requested information unless the person lacks capacity to cooperate.SB413-SSA1,6,95(3) Immunity for victim. A victim of a sexual assault is immune from being 6charged or prosecuted under s. 961.573 for the possession of drug paraphernalia, 7under s. 961.41 (3g) for the possession of a controlled substance or a controlled 8substance analog, and under s. 961.69 (2) for possession of a masking agent if all of 9the following apply:SB413-SSA1,6,1210(a) The victim reported or disclosed the sexual assault with the intent to 11obtain assistance for the sexual assault or was in need of assistance due to the 12sexual assault.SB413-SSA1,6,1413(b) The evidence for the charge or prosecution was obtained as a result of the 14victim’s action described under par. (a).SB413-SSA1,6,1715(4) Prohibition on revocation. A person’s pretrial release, probation, 16extended supervision, parole, or supervised release may not be revoked based on an 17incident for which the person is immune from prosecution under sub. (2) or (3).SB413-SSA1,6,2118(5) Exception. This section does not apply to a person who reports or 19discloses an alleged sexual assault with an intention to claim the protections under 20this section knowing that the fact situation that he or she reports or discloses does 21not exist.SB413-SSA1,1322Section 13. Initial applicability.SB413-SSA1,7,223(1) Notwithstanding s. 990.06, the treatment of s. 939.74 (2) (ar) first applies 1to an act for which the time limit under s. 939.74 (2) (ar), 2023 stats., for 2prosecution has not expired as of the effective date of this subsection.SB413-SSA1,7,33(end) Down Down /2025/related/amendments/sb413/ssa1_sb413 true amends /2025/related/amendments/sb413/ssa1_sb413 amends/2025/REG/SB413-SSA1 amends/2025/REG/SB413-SSA1 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Amendments»SB413»Amendment SSA1-SB413 × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
