SB413-SSA1,128Section 12. 961.444 of the statutes is created to read:
SB413-SSA1,5,129961.444 Person seeking assistance following sexual assault;
10immunity from prosecution. (1) Definition. In this section, “sexual assault”
11means conduct that is in violation of s. 940.22 (2), 940.225, 940.302, or 944.06 or a
12crime under ch. 948 that involves sexual contact or sexually explicit conduct.
SB413-SSA1,5,1713(2) Immunity for aider. A person is immune from being charged or
14prosecuted under s. 961.573 for the possession of drug paraphernalia, under s.
15961.41 (3g) for the possession of a controlled substance or a controlled substance
16analog, and under s. 961.69 (2) for possession of a masking agent if all of the
17following apply:
SB413-SSA1,5,2318(a) The person requested emergency assistance in connection with a sexual
19assault by calling the telephone number “911” or by other means, the person
20encountered a law enforcement officer at a medical facility at the time that the
21victim of the sexual assault received treatment in connection with the sexual
22assault, or the person reported or disclosed the sexual assault with the intent to
23obtain assistance for the sexual assault.
SB413-SSA1,6,2
1(b) The evidence for the charge or prosecution is obtained as a result of the
2person’s action described under par. (a).
SB413-SSA1,6,43(c) The person provides a name and contact information and furnishes all
4requested information unless the person lacks capacity to cooperate.
SB413-SSA1,6,95(3) Immunity for victim. A victim of a sexual assault is immune from being
6charged or prosecuted under s. 961.573 for the possession of drug paraphernalia,
7under s. 961.41 (3g) for the possession of a controlled substance or a controlled
8substance analog, and under s. 961.69 (2) for possession of a masking agent if all of
9the following apply:
SB413-SSA1,6,1210(a) The victim reported or disclosed the sexual assault with the intent to
11obtain assistance for the sexual assault or was in need of assistance due to the
12sexual assault.
SB413-SSA1,6,1413(b) The evidence for the charge or prosecution was obtained as a result of the
14victim’s action described under par. (a).
SB413-SSA1,6,1715(4) Prohibition on revocation. A person’s pretrial release, probation,
16extended supervision, parole, or supervised release may not be revoked based on an
17incident for which the person is immune from prosecution under sub. (2) or (3).
SB413-SSA1,6,2118(5) Exception. This section does not apply to a person who reports or
19discloses an alleged sexual assault with an intention to claim the protections under
20this section knowing that the fact situation that he or she reports or discloses does
21not exist.
